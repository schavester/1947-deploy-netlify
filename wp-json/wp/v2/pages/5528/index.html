Lynn Peril is obsessed with used books (particularly old sex and dating manuals, etiquette and self-help books and health, beauty and fashion guides) and other detritus of popular culture, especially that concerning gender-related behavioral prescriptions. <\/p>\n

Lynn Peril \u00e2\u20ac\u0153has tirelessly chronicled and analyzed mid-20th-century examples of what she calls \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcpink think: a set of ideas and attitudes about what constituted proper female behavior \u00e2\u20ac\u00a6 [that are] earnest, kitschy and offensive.\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 She started earning street cred in the field with her 1990s \u00e2\u20ac\u02dczine Mystery Date, which showcased women\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s advice books, and then with her long-time \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcMuseum of Femorabilia\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 column in Bust Magazine. . . . Peril\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s true delight in her research comes through in every page. She knows how to mine the humor in the truly horrifying.\u00e2\u20ac\u009d<\/p>\n
