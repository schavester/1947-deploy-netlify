March 31, 1927
\nPasadena<\/p>\n

Fourteen year-old Wilbur Garner<\/a> had a lady-friend, and an older one at that, his inamoratette a fifteen year-old Eula Rittgers<\/a>. They showed great attachment to one another at their Seventh Day Adventist School<\/a>. When they decided to exchange biblical dullsville for the world\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s treasures, they outfoxed the Man by turning li\u00e2\u20ac\u2122l Eula into a boy. Inside a church\u00c2\u00a0 wastebasket was found the Eula’s hair, and persons conforming to the two young boys’ description were spotted in Eagle Rock. A fashionable bobbed ‘do meets a Joan of Arc act. Appropriately observant.\u00c2\u00a0 Guess they were absent the day they covered Deuteronomy 22:5.<\/p>\n

In yet more<\/em> fifteen year-old news, or, that is to say, further news of fifteen year-olds, fifteen year-old Jimmy Davis<\/a> and an unnamed pal of his broke into the Monterey Park home of John W. Hardman<\/a>, stealing fountain pens and trinkets and, more absurdly, did Jimmy garb himself in Hardman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s best suit, silk shirt and black & white scarf. Figuring himself too conspicuous for his own good, Jimmy and pal returned to the house, threw the clothes on the floor and, afraid of being traced through fingerprints (his being known to local authorities for his repeated burglaries and check forgings), lit fire to the house. The house smoldered for some time before being rescued by the fire department, and Jimmy is now cooling his heels in juvenile hall.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
