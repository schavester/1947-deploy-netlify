February 17, 1927
Santa Ana<\/p>\n

The widow Alice Hanmore has a bone to pick with Evangelists<\/a>, or, more specifically, the College of Medical Evangelists<\/a>.  Truth be told, evangelists should be, oh, evangelical, and leave the application of Röntgen rays to the professionals.  <\/p>\n

In March of 1926 Alice’s husband M. J. Hanmore, a Fullerton oil worker, began experiencing stomach pains and loss of appetite; Drs. Claude E. Steen, Emerald J. Steen and John A. Whalen of the CME\/White Memorial Hospital<\/a> decided that an intensive course of that ever-beneficial ionizing radiation would do the trick.  Today, Alice is charging in court that “negligent and unskillful” employment of X-rays resulted in severe fatal burns—she’s asking for $30,000 ($348,669 USD2007).<\/p>\n

(Our evangelical docs Steen & Steen will make the papers again in March, charged of malpractice by one Mary A. Greene of Fullerton—she goes in for an ingrown toenail, so they take that portion of the nail.  So far so good.  Steen & Steen subsequently amputate her big toe.  Then<\/em> they amputate much of her leg.  Further operations result in anthropy of Mary’s thigh muscles.  She’ll ask for $25,000.)<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
