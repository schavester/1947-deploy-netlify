Feb. 24, 1907
\nEagle Rock<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Architect Samuel Tilden Norton has designed a bank building for Townsend Avenue and Colorado Boulevard<\/a> in Eagle Rock, The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Just to make research interesting, The Times misspelled his name as S. Tilton Norton. According to his 1959 obituary, his mother was the first Jewish child born in Los Angeles. After studying architecture in Los Angeles and New York, he designed the Wilshire Fox Building and Sinai Temple. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

He was a board member of Temple B\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2nai <\/span>Brith during its move from Hope and 9th<\/sup> Street to Wilshire and Hobart and was an honorary consultant on plans for the temple, which was designed by A.W. Edelman. <\/span><\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Norton was lifelong friends with Rabbi Edgar F. Magnin, who presided over the funeral. Norton was a member of many professional, religious and social organizations, including Hillcrest Country Club, Nathan Strauss Israel Society, Jewish Federation, the Friends of the Hebrew University and the Zionist Organization of America. <\/p>\n
