July 13, 1907
Los Angeles <\/p>\n

And you thought the tong war was all wrapped up (cf. our post of 6 July). That uneasy easy peace you felt must sadly be disturbed once more. <\/p>\n

Chan Mon is one of the wealthiest and most influential Celestials in Los Angeles. He resides in Chinatown at 212 Ferguson Alley, and runs from 804 Juan Street his empire of vegetable wholesalers. <\/p>\n

But at a banquet (at 421 Apablasa) last night—you’ll remember that the last tong war resulted from a banquet, where some ladies of a particular social standing were omitted—Chan Mon, a fellow traveler of the Hop Sing, was shot once in the shoulder and twice in the groin by members of the Row Wing and Bow On. <\/p>\n

Police were dispatched to the train stations to prevent escape of the assassins, and over one hundred officers ransacked Chinatown in a house-to-house. They never found noted highbinder Lem Au Toy, implicated in the shooting, but did arrest Lee Ming, a tailor and Hop Sing hanger-on, at 214 Apablasa. <\/p>\n

Barring blood poisoning, Mon is expected to recover. <\/p>\n

We will keep you posted of local developments.<\/p>\n

An interlude from the future: <\/p>\n

Baravelli and Pinky arrive carrying blocks of ice. They deposit them in the wall safe.<\/em> Wagstaff: That’s a fine way to carry ice! Where are your tongs? Baravelli and Pinky stick out their tounges.<\/em> Looks like a tong war. <\/p>\n

— Horsefeathers<\/em>, 1932 <\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
