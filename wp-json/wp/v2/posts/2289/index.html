\nMarch 16, 1927
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

If the drys are gonna catch the wets, they\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2re gonna have to wet themselves. So to speak.\n<\/p>\n

\nAt the trial of John H. Wyncoop, former chief field agent for the boys of the California\/Arizona Federal Prohibition Enforcement Department, Wyncoop said \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cI knew that if I had liquor in my possession I could more easily get bootleggers to believe that I was handling booze and therefore make it easier to arrest bootleggers.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

Uh-huh<\/em>.<\/p>\n

Wyncoop is on trial because he turned twenty-nine bottles of liquor to his own use, instead of turning it into the government warehouse. Can\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t those government know-nothings see that you need that hooch to go under deep cover<\/a>? That he only took home that demon rum<\/a> in the solemn performance of his duty?<\/p>\n

(Convicted by a jury of illegal conversion, he was given a short term in the county jail.)<\/p>\n
