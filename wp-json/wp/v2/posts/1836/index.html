<\/a>Jan. 11, 1907
\n<\/span>Los Angeles
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span>A woman living on a hog ranch near the Santa Fe railroad crossing over the Los Angeles River contacted police after seeing dismembered human bodies in the old dumping ground near George Street.
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span>Investigators dug through the dump, retrieving the body of a child that was nearly intact, along with bits and pieces of a man and a woman, including their skulls. In addition to the remains, police found books and papers traced to the University of Southern California Medical School.
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span> \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cWhoever is responsible for the depositing of the remains on the garbage heap should be severely censured,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Coroner Roy S. Lanterman<\/a><\/span> told The Times.
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cIt seems quite heartless enough to give up the human body to further science but when the students have finished dissecting the remains they should see that they are interred with the proper respect. I cannot understand the action of those responsible for sending the bodies to the garbage heap.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d
\n<\/span>
\n<\/span>For further reading on the sorry state of medical schools at the turn of the 20th century, read Abraham Flexner\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cMedical Education in the U.S. and Canada<\/a><\/span>.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Note that in this era, medical students didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t even need to be high school graduates.
