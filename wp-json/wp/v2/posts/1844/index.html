<\/a><\/a>Jan. 17, 1907
\nLos Angeles <\/p>\n

On a trip from Utah to visit his daughter, H.E. Gibson keeps getting lost as he wanders around Los Angeles. No, it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s not because Gibson is 80, for his mind is still sharp. It\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s because he hasn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t been back since 1848 and things have changed just a bit. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Even the old familiar landmark of Ft. Hill is covered with homes, he says. About the only spot in town he recognizes is the Plaza, where he keeps returning to get his bearings. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Gibson <\/span>came to California with the \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cFlash Emigrant Colony\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d to establish Mormon settlements. The group couldn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t raise the money to buy Rancho Cucamonga, so they bought a parcel of land in San Bernardino, The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Land was \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cdirt cheap\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d in 1848, with entire blocks selling for $500 to $1,000, ($9,910.34-$19,820.69 USD 2005), Gibson said. <\/span>But instead of becoming a real estate speculator, he left for Utah to bring the news (published in a New York newspaper that came around the Horn) proclaiming that Brigham Young had been appointed governor of the Utah Territory. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Note: Today presented a difficult decision, but I passed on some incredibly offensive caricatures of an African American who had been arrested, accompanied by quotes in dialect: <\/span>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cAh dunno nothin\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 about no stolen chickens\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d indeed. <\/span><\/p>\n
