December 22, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Mrs. Helen Miller, 19, met a man in a restaurant about a week ago, and agreed to go back to his hotel room. He told her his name was Donald Graeff, and if she thought she might forget it, any chance was lost after he held her captive and carved his initials into her upper thigh with a dull jacknife. “I am going to brand you,” he explained, “So I can keep you all to myself.”<\/p>\n

Today, Mrs. Miller managed to get word out to police, and was rescued. Mr. Graeff is in police custody, and will be questioned about several unsolved sex\/mutilation murders in the city, including that of Elizabeth Short, the Black Dahlia.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
