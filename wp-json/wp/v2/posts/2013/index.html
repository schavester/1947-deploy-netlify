June 30, 1927
\nFullerton<\/p>\n

Two members of the Ralph Ince Film Company<\/a> returned to the California Hotel<\/a> in Fullerton ’round midnight tonight to find their fearless leader, Ralph Ince<\/a>, semiconscious and supine upon the floor.\u00c2\u00a0 Nipping the ol\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 Hollywood joy juice down in Valenciaville, eh, Ralphie?<\/p>\n

Why, no!\u00c2\u00a0 He\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s been the victim of the Mad Gasser of Fullerton<\/a>!\u00c2\u00a0 Hotel resident Carl Breusch said he’d seen a man skulking about the corridor, carrying a can, and that said can-carrier leapt out of a window when approached.\u00c2\u00a0 Guests Charles Scott and Charles McMaster were awakened in their respective bedrooms by the odor of the anesthetic solvent and then espied through their windows a shadowy figure running down the street.<\/p>\n

Though the papers reported Alois Sabinski’s recent battle with chloroform in his Nicholas Street home, California Hotel lessee Ellen Lincoln declared she’d heard nothing about any \u00e2\u20ac\u0153chloroform burglar;\u00e2\u20ac\u009d Fullerton Chief of Police T. K. Winter said, ahem, reports regarding any such character have been greatly exaggerated.<\/p>\n

In any event, Ince has departed for his company\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s location in Santa Ana Canyon, and can not be reached for comment.<\/p>\n
