\n
November 6, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

When asked why he had attempted to kidnap Celina Jarmillo, 18, as she was leaving work at 1427 E. Fourth Street<\/a>, Raymond Adame, also 18, explained: “Last April she made me a sandwich of potatoes, beans and macaroni, and, according to our legends, she bewitched me. I couldn’t get out of her spell.” A later reports added fish eyes to the sandwich ingredients, and Farmer’s Market columnist Fred Beek suggested this might be a good addition to a meatless Tuesday menu.<\/p>\n

In any case, Celina’s witchery must still have been working, because a radio car drove by just as Raymond tried to make the snatch. He’s down at Hollenbeck cooling his heels. Adame usually resides at 206 N. Clarence Street, Jarmillo at 5927 Fifth Ave.<\/p>\n
