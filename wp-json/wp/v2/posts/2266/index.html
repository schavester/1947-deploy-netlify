February 16, 1927
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Those junkies and hopheads that once provided the occasional bruise on this perfect ripe plum that is Los Angeles—shall be no more!  Though alcoholism was cured in 1908<\/a>, drug addiction still remains to blight the landscape.  But Narcosan has arrived to save the day!  <\/p>\n

Drs. E. H. Anthony and Benjamin Blank, their committee of peace officers and other physicians in tow, have at their disposal the first shipment from New York of this new European wonder drug.  <\/p>\n

Any addicted Angeleno can trot down to Blank’s offices in the Quinby Building<\/a>, Seventh and Grand, and take the cure free of charge.  They’ve got fifteen addicts lined up to undergo treatment and are looking to administer to at least another ten, so get down there you, you narc-addled fiend!<\/p>\n

(Despite liberal Narcosan administration to the lucky souls who so evidently deserved it<\/a>,  apparently the wonder drug didn’t work out so well<\/a>.) <\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
