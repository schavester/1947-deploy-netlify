Hollywood<\/p>\n

Homeowners, it’s like this: you own your home<\/span>; the sidewalk and the curb and the street belong to everybody!<\/p>\n

Earl Richard Casper, I’m talking to you. What were you thinking, hovering around in front of your pad at 833 N. Cahuenga<\/a>, arguing with Ted Stanhope<\/a> (45, occupation: thespian) over how he was parking his car? Turning your hose<\/span> on Stanhope’s engine, killing it and ensuring that both he and the offending machine would remain right there–the former now in a state of indignation? And blasting Stanhope<\/span> with the hose when he got out of his car to protest?<\/p>\n

Casper, you’re lucky he just rassled with you on the lawn, took the hose away and called the cops. Next time, can’t you just be a normal<\/span> uptight freak, lurk behind the curtains and mouth bad words?<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
