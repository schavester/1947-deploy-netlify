May 30, 1947
Hollywood<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cPsst! Olivia de Havilland! \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cOperations Everything\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d is off!\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

That\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s the happy news police gave today to the Academy award-winning actress, with the arrest of her self-styled advisor, Paul Randall, a 34-year-old artist from Chicago who has been inundating de Havilland with unsolicited advice letters signed \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cSt. Paul\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d or with a creepy P-in-a-circle motif.<\/p>\n

The letters were followed by recent telegrams announcing Randall\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s imminent arrival in Los Angeles and demanding de Havilland meet him \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201calone.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Today he angrily phoned de Havilland\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s agent Kurt Frints to protest her failure to meet him at Municipal Airport<\/a>. He told Frints that he was in town to carry out \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cOperations Everything,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and that Miss de Havilland, whom he had met twelve years ago when they were doing theatrical work (in Van Nuys, no less), would understand what he meant. He intended to wait at 1738 N. Las Palmas Avenue<\/a>, where \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cshe better see me.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

Instead it was detectives who came to take a look at St. Paul in his new digs in Hollywood Jail. They wired Chicago for more information, and asked that police psychiatrist Dr. Paul De River examine him. Olivia de Havilland denies knowing her visitor.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
