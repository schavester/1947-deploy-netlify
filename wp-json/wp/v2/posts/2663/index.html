<\/a>July 19, 1947
El Monte<\/p>\n

When traffic officers busted 14-year-old Leonard Kiter of 521 S. Muscatel Avenue, San Gabriel last night for jumping a signal on his motor scooter at Five Points in El Monte,<\/a> they opened up a hornet\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s nest. For it seems young Leonard was carrying the wallet of his grandfather William, and could give police and his father Leland no explanation for it.<\/p>\n

Leland was concerned, and went with officers to the campsite near Big Bear<\/a> where the 70-year-old was staying (in town, he lived at 2511 Las Flores Ave., Alhambra<\/a>). There they found William Kiter, dead from axe wounds, laying on a cot.<\/p>\n

The boy initially denied knowledge of his grandfather\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s death, but soon confessed to killing him. They had spent the morning fishing, and the old man was mad about a lost anchor and anchor rope. Before that, there were fights over William\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s refusal to rent horses and motorbikes for Leonard. So when William went to take a nap, Leonard crept up and whacked him.<\/p>\n

Suddenly helpful, Leonard showed police where he hid both parts of the axe, about a quarter mile away from the camp site, in two places. And he told them how before the killing, he\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d carefully placed a blanket over his little dog to spare the creature the terrible sight.*<\/p>\n

suggested reading: Without Conscience: The Disturbing World of the Psychopaths Among Us<\/a> <\/b>by Robert D. Hare <\/span><\/a><\/p>\n

[* a note from 2005: Leonard was charged with murder the next day, and not a single mention of the boy or his crime ever again appeared in the pages of the L.A. Times.]<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
