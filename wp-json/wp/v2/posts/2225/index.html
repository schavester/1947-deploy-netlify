January 6, 1927
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Gladys Nolan, 22, of 5510 Lexington Avenue<\/a>, had a craving for fine clothes and expensive perfumes.  She needed them.  Yes, there’s a difference between needs and wants.  She NEEDED them.  <\/p>\n

Gladys was no klepto.  She paid for the items, and not with money from the handbag of some white-glove spinster she’d clobbered and left twitching in her death throes down a urine-soaked alley.  Gladys paid for these things with all the nicety befitting a girl of refinement, trouble being, she paid for the lovely things with forged checks.<\/p>\n

A $200 ($2,206 USD 2007) fur coat and $34 bottle of perfume, she picked up at I<\/a>. Magnin’s<\/a>; a check signed in a fictitious name at Maison Blanche<\/a> allowed her a gown and hat totaling $110.  Some killjoy by the name of “Deputy District Attoney Frampton” got in a twist about this, convincing some other sourpuss called “Judge Ambrose” to hold her to answer in Superior Court and fix bail at $2000.<\/p>\n

Gladys was given probation and told to keep her nose clean.  Which she almost did.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Whatever became of Gladys Nolan?  A lady whose refinement and obvious taste sadly outdistanced her pocketbook?  Guess we’ll never know.<\/p>\n
