March 6, 2007
\nSan Francisco (VIA Associated Press)<\/p>\n

<\/o:p> <\/p>\n

The Irish of San Francisco are furious over a play at the Davis Theater called \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe Belle of Avenue A<\/a>,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d which features a character named Mrs. McCluskey who drinks a glass of beer in the first act. <\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThree times, about 40 people charged the stage and the actors and actresses feared they were about to be attacked,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. <\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cIndignant because the woman\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s part in the first act called for the drinking of a glass of beer, two score men, members of the Irish societies of this city, charged the stage and for half an hour refused to allow the play to go on.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

The riot was reported to the police and the protesters were eventually thrown out of the theater. <\/p>\n
