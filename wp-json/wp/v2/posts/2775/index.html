10 May was a slow news day for crime. But as I put my feet up and vainly perused the paper for evidence of murder and mayhem, an item about a new house caught my eye. Here in 1947, where building costs are high and the housing shortage acute, veteran Clement Cointe has decided to build a miniature dream house, to live in until such time as he can enlarge.<\/p>\n

Enlisting architects Harold Bissner and Harold Zook, they\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ve come up with a 700sf one-room house with a magnificently modern wall of glass (please note how unobtrusively it fits into the landscape):<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Their one room is all-purpose, with closets that open to reveal built in linen and dish storage, card table, bar, coat closet and radio-phonograph. The Harolds included built in bookshelves and desk. The bedroom area\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dcouches of grey upholstery with Mexican cow pony pillows\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dcan be partitioned off with an accordion wall. The whitewashed fireplace in the wall of rough-sawed redwood stakes is a nice touch.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Oh, hell. Since I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ve brought up the housing shortage, I might as well touch on one of LA\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s juicier housing stories: in the late 40s the City Housing Authority, its coffers full of federal lucre, pulls an eminent domain on the people of Chavez Ravine. Out come the bulldozers, which level the barrio community. Don\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t worry,<\/em> says the Authority, we\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2re building you a 3,300 unit high-rise housing project, and you\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll all get first pick of the brand new units! (And it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s designed by Richard-Freakin\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2-Neutra no less!)<\/p>\n

But uh-oh, here comes the Committee Against Socialist Housing. And here comes HUAC hot on their heels. In 1953 new mayor Poulson kills the whole deal, including the resident\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Right of Return. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153Round about that time O\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2Malley couldn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t connive an Ebbets Field replacement out of the borough of Brooklyn, so he went fishing in LA, and found a sweetheart deal if ever there was one. You know the rest. Go Dodger Blue.<\/p>\n

Not that I don\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t love Captain Emil Praeger\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s park\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dthe early 60s stadium is replete with atom-age flourishes\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dbut I hope no-one goes there without recalling this image:<\/p>\n
