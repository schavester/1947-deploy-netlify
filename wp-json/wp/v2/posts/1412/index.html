Feb. 7, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n


\n<\/span><\/h1>\n

A Child’s Testimony<\/span>\n<\/p>\n

Charles Babbitt is sentenced to 30 days in jail on charges of domestic violence after the testimony of his 6-year-old son. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cPapa hit me with a whip and it cut my head,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d the boy said. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThen he hit mama.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/span>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe man blinked his eyes and said that he did it because he was drunk\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says.<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Ross’ Widow Arrested<\/p>\n

Mary Ross, whose husband was killed by Officer Hoover, is fined $50 after being arrested in a raid on a rooming house that was selling liquor without a license. Ross was among the women seized at the establishment of Mrs. Mary Cooper, 261\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00bd S. Los Angeles St. William Ross<\/a>, who fatally shot Officer C.A May, was buried in potter\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s field, The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Fined for Blind Pig<\/p>\n

Frank Stadler pleads guilty to running a blind pig called the Mechanics Club, 1466 Channing St., and is ordered to pay a $50 fine. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Chinese Lottery Case<\/p>\n

E.S. Patton is sent to jail after failing to pay a $50 fine for selling Chinese lottery tickets. Patton is the first white man to be fined for such sales, The Times says.<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

A Familiar Face\n<\/p>\n

Patrol officers recognized J.W. Mason, who had just gotten out of jail, and watched as he found \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201ca drunken, well-dressed man and lured him into a doorway,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. He was given 20 days in jail for disorderly conduct.<\/p>\n
