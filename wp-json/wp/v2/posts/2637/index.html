August 8, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

ACLU representative A.L. Wirin, an attorney, is asking Superior Judge Henry M. Willis to rule that those recent \u00c3\u201a\u00e2\u20ac\u0153crime crusher\u00c3\u201a\u00e2\u20ac\u009d street blockades<\/a> are unconstitutional and must be stopped. The search and seizure of property belonging to people who just happen to be driving through a blockaded intersection is, Wirin states, \u00c3\u201a\u00e2\u20ac\u0153intolerable and unreasonable.\u00c3\u201a\u00e2\u20ac\u009d<\/p>\n

Police claim a decrease of 18.2 percent in reported crimes in the blockades sections of the city, and seek to continue the practice. In an affidavit filed at the Court, Assistant Chief of Police Joseph Reed insisted that only cars containing \u00c3\u201a\u00e2\u20ac\u0153suspicious characters\u00c3\u201a\u00e2\u20ac\u009d were being searched, and that for law-abiding citizens, the stop was no more inconvenient than a traffic light. A side issue is the fact that public funds are being used to finance the blockades.<\/p>\n

Judge Willis took the case under submission.<\/p>\n
