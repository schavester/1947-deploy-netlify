March 2, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Cabbie Clifford Brown is fortunate to be alive tonight after an encounter with an armed robber at 110th and Central<\/a>. A fare asked him to wait there while he picked up a buddy, but the buddy came packing heat. Before Brown had a chance to respond, the gunman’s finger twitched, and a bullet tore into the cabbie’s breast pocket. The pocket was stuffed full of change, which made an improvised shield as its contents flew wildly away from the shocked victim. The original fare, a cool sorta cucumber, suggested, “Don’t be so nervous there, Joe. Get down and pick that money off the floor.”<\/p>\n

Brown, who lives at 1683 1\/2 Palm Lane, lost $58 and was bruised around the chest, but is otherwise unharmed.<\/p>\n
