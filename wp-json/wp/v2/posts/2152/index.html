October 27, 1927
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Of course we know that pigtails have gone the way of the dodo for the li’l ‘uns, torn asunder by the terrible rake of modernity, but we never thought the bob<\/em> would be stomped extinct by carnality and venality, modernity’s ugly little twins.  But the bob is gone!  Long live the permanent wave!<\/p>\n

Here, five year-old Dottie Landvogt, of the Seventy-Sixty Street Landvogts, sits at Johanna’s Permanent Wave Shop in the Pantages Building on Broadway.  She’s hooked up to one of those Charles Nessler<\/a> contraptions—a crazy of octopus holding two-pound brass rollers on a system of counterweights, suspended from a chandelier; the curlers, filled with a Borax paste reagent, are electrically heated to 200+ degrees to boil and steam the hair and, because Nessler didn’t have a shop in Los Angeles, this was probably one of the many cheap knock-off machines.  Which meant Dot likely ended up with a nicely burned scalp to match her ‘do.<\/p>\n

But such is the price of beauty.<\/p>\n

By the time Dorothy is ten, heatless waves will be all the rage, so we wish her the best of luck in still having hair. <\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
