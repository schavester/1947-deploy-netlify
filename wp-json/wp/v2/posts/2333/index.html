February 18, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

All evidence suggests that, while driving west on Washington Boulevard under the overpass near Santa Fe Avenue, trucker Robert Frazer was struck by a piece of falling concrete. He then drove more than eight miles, in heavy traffic, before careening onto the sidewalk at his place of employ, at Firestone and Paramount Blvds.<\/a> Co-workers rushed to his aid, and found Frazer unconscious, with a wound over his eye.
