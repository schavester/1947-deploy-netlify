<\/a>August 2, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

The crows and ravens of the Santa Barbara orange and walnut groves cower on the wing before the 20-, 16- and 12-gauge shotguns of Richey Haddon, bloodthirsty claimant of more bounties on the pesky avians than any other Californian. Fish and Game pays 15 cents a bird, so Haddon shoots them out of the sky–4681 since the bounty was enacted in September 1945 (that\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s $213.45 in crow cash).<\/p>\n

Haddon doesn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t just shoot crows\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dhe eats<\/span> them, and sleeps on a mattress stuffed with their pin feathers.<\/p>\n

The Killer of Crows lives at 1209 W. 38th Street<\/a>, an address migrating birds will want to avoid (or perhaps crap on, under cover of night).<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
