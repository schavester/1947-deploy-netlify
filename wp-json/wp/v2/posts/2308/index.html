March 9, 1947
Santa Ana<\/p>\n

Flummoxed partway through the ride home to El Toro Marine Base, when the Negro cabbie suggested he come up front and take the wheel as he was feeling sleepy, Patrick Crawford accomodated the snoring driver by delivering him straight to the Santa Ana cops. It was a good instinct on the young Marine’s part: investigation revealed that sleepy Mervin Wilturner, 21, was likely the fare who had shot the cab’s real owner, Beverly J. Barton, twice in the head earlier this evening. Barton is in critical condition in French Hospital, while Wilturner, a transient, is being held at Newton Street Police Station on suspicion of assault with intent to commit murder.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
