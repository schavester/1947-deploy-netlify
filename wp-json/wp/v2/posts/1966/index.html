May 28, 1927
Riverside <\/p>\n

"I guess the only way to stop divorce is to stop marriage."<\/em> — Will Rogers<\/a> <\/p>\n

Everyone is familiar with the Boy Scout<\/a> motto, Be Prepared, but newlyweds Grant Dewlaney and Ethel Hornaday have prepared themselves for marriage in a way that no Boy Scout ever could have imagined. <\/p>\n

Grant and Ethel hope to spend the rest of their lives together in blissful tandem harness, but what if the unthinkable happens and they fall out of love like the ten couples who filed for divorce today in Los Angeles? <\/p>\n

In the event that their love does not endure, the pragmatic pair has taken an unusual step to avoid future battles over the house, furniture, and the family Ford<\/a>. Prior to their marriage ceremony they filed an agreement with the Los Angeles County Recorder which may inspire other modern couples to do the same. <\/p>\n

The contract<\/a> states that if Ethel ever files for divorce she will accept a settlement of $500 ($5939.25USD 2007) to pay for an attorney, her separate maintenance and any other of her expenses. If Ethel files for divorce before their first wedding anniversary, she has agreed to accept the sum of $300 ($3563.55USD 2007). <\/p>\n

Will this type of agreement ever catch on? Only time will tell. <\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
