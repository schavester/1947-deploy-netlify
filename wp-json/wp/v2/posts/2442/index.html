December 19, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Nurse Fay Young, 28, was dressed all in black when they found her in a cafe<\/a> two blocks from her apartment at 826 W. Sixth Street–down to the .45 caliber Army automatic hidden in her purse.<\/p>\n

Police were interested, because Fay matched the description of the woman who had just held up Stanley Brown, 1110 S. Lake Street, for $9 nearby. Would that she had walked to the cafe. It was her suspicious behavior in a cab that led driver Sam Wurtzel, 1163 S. Kingsley, to drop a dime on her. It seems she had been cradling the weapon in her lap and cooing to it, “This is my only friend, my best friend.”<\/p>\n
