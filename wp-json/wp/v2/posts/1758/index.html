Nov.12, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Half a block from his home at 1131 Westlake<\/a>, John P. Shumway Jr. was badly injured when the carriage he was driving collided with the 11th<\/sup> Street trolley. <\/span>Shumway was thrown about 20 feet, striking the pavement head-first, and the horse ran for the stable, pulling what was left of the smashed carriage, witnesses said. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Shumway was carried to his home, where his father, Dr. John P. Shumway, treated him for a concussion, bruises and cuts. A year later, the family filed a personal injury suit against the Los Angeles Railway, seeking $10,355 ($204,938.83), although The Times failed to report the outcome of the trial.<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Whether Shumway was a troublesome sort before is unclear, but his problems continued. In 1909, he was arrested for passing a forged check for $25 at the Pioneer bar on North Main Street. He claimed that he was given the check for work he had performed and was freed when he promised to repay the money. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n
