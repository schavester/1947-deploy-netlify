March 26, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Seven major West Los Angeles and Hollywood intersections were blockaded tonight, and passing cars searched, as part of Assistant Police Chief Joseph F. Reed’s “crime crushers” campaign. Wilshire, University and Hollywood Division Police arrested 67 suspected criminals at these stops, among them 31 robbers, 9 car thieves and 5 carriers of concealed weapons.<\/p>\n

Curious about the lack of traffic at the Melrose \/ La Brea<\/a> blockade, police investigated and found Thomas A. Reeves, 23, of 1200 S. Windsor Blvd.<\/a>, standing at the corner of Melrose and Rossmore Aves.<\/a> holding a sign reading ROAD BLOCK AT MELROSE AND LA BREA. <\/span><\/p>\n

While being booked at City Jail for public drunkenness, our hero reported that someone had given him a dollar to hold the sign. Meanwhile, Police claimed success for their campaign, noting that only one robbery was reported citywide, as opposed to eight or nine for a typical Tuesday night.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
