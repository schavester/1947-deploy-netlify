1947\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009da lot of women-killing, a lot of booze. It\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s enough to turn one into a teetotaling sub. Almost.<\/p>\n

And here, a woman killing herself. With booze. Nowadays, her family would call up A&E and she\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d be on Intervention<\/span>. Perfect fodder for the show\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dsomeone: somewhere once, nowhere now. Our identified family member has hit bottom. Get them into treatment. God, give me the strength to blame those who did this to me, to accuse those who didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t, and the wisdom to know the difference…a lifetime of coffee, cigarettes and forced clapping after each and every utterance. <\/p>\n

Evelyn Winters was described as \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cbrilliant\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d by those who knew her, a legal eagle for the studio system since she was 23, til her alcholism caught up with her and she was shitcanned from the film colony at 37. Was there sensitivity training in the workplace for those who still suffer? This is 1947. The only place you\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll be happy, joyous and free is in the afterlife. For more information about alcohol, ask a parent or teacher! Or go here<\/a>.<\/p>\n

The elephant in the copy room went to the elephant graveyard: skid row. Where does a homeless 800-lb. gorilla sleep? Anywhere it can. And so forth.<\/p>\n

Evelyn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s last known address\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dSeptember, 1946\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dwas here, at 2822 Rowena:
<\/p>\n

But in the months prior to her assault and murder she had been living in the beer parlors on Hill and Figueroa, keeping what was left of her belongings in a liquor store.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

She was out carousing, divorced, jobless, though with, I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d wager, a mind still keen and ticking, before she was found nearly nude, beaten, and dragged for some way, near the Ducommun Street railroad right-of-way, here:
<\/p>\n

Evelyn, homeless, now has a homeless encampment on her site.<\/p>\n

So, then there\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s this Tiernan character. <\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

He\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s twelve years Evelyn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s junior. A former employee of the Angelus Bowling and Billiard Recreation Center\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009d
<\/p>\n

which is now a parking lot:
<\/p>\n

(for more on prewar bowling alleys, go here<\/a>)<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dhe takes Evelyn to the Albany Hotel at 912 W. Sixth. He drinks with her there for a day and change and, if he is to be believed, she departs between 7:30 and 8pm. She is found at 12:10am.<\/p>\n

The Albany, where she may have had her last drink, or did not, is gone:<\/p>\n


(Sanwa Bank Plaza, AC Martin, 1990)<\/p>\n

Never did find a vintage image of the Albany\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u00a6some flavor of the wiped-out neighborhood\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009done block west:
<\/p>\n

And one block east:
<\/p>\n

But why the hotel room? We don\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t know. Tiernan didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t live at the Albany. He lived at the Armondale, at 728 South Flower.
<\/p>\n

Its site today:
<\/p>\n

First off, what, already, is up with the Armondale Hotel? It has that \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cbuilt on Indian burial ground\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d cachet that money can\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t buy. Perhaps it was simply built over one of those giant magnets. The kind that attract ne\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2er-do-wells.<\/p>\n

The place had trouble attached from the get-go. Dale Carleton, developer and proprietor of the spanking-new 1914 Armondale, is sued by wifey Marie for a sizable share of his $250,000 net worth. Mrs. Carleton names a Ms. Helen Williams\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dArmondale telephone girl whose duties apparently went above and beyond the working of switchboard\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009das correspondent. <\/p>\n

1919. Wilbert Garrison, 28, son of a wealthy publisher in New York, drove across country with a buddy and they holed up in the Armondale. A week later Wilbert left in his room his money, valuables, and a note indicating that he did not want to be a burden on others, and as such was ending his life. Despite the best efforts of the Nick Harris detective agency (who calls the cops in 1919?), Wilbert is never found.<\/p>\n

1930. Mrs. Louis Valenzuella, 40\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dex-wife of Deputy Sheriff Valenzuella\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dis found dead in the Armondale of a suspected drug overdose.<\/p>\n

1939. Washed-up boxer Louis Menney, 22, Armondale resident, is tackled by a priest after he sexually assaulted a 62 year-old woman in a church at 9th (now James M. Woods) and Green. Turns out he\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dmoletsed? raped?\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dthe papers will only mention \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cmorals offenses\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009da nine year-old in the church as well. Moreover, he\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d done his business with a six year-old girl on the corner of 11th (now Chick Hearn) and Georgia, and also kidnapped and robbed an Agnes M—– and sexually assaulted a Margaret L—– in a church on West Adams; since the kidnapping charge is death penalty territory, we can only hope the Armondale\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s most famous resident ended up in the proper hands.
<\/p>\n

1948. Francis Sylvester, of the Armondale, works across the street at the Western Union at 741 South Flower. Sylvester wires untold sums in care of himself to small outlying towns, where there are no Western Union offices, and destroys the records of the transactions.<\/p>\n

And 1965. Percy Hatch, 65, who had been in the hotel since 1957, started talking crazy-talk. As in, a loggorhea of obscenities for two straight weeks. Behind the Armondale registration desk was manager Nancy Furlow, 62, who, finally fed up with her repeated warnings, reached for the phone, and was shot dead by Hatch with one bullet. Hatch therewith turned the gun on himself.<\/p>\n

Shortly thereafter the Armondale was felled and a rather ill-advised Broadway was built on the site. Now a Macy\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s, it resembles a Dawn<\/span> mall on a slow day. For more on this exercise in brown, please go here<\/a>.<\/p>\n

Tiernan had been reading with Evelyn at Central Library for a couple years. They would read, or shack up and drink, and maybe he’d talk bowling and maybe she’d talk law, but probably not. Neither he nor anyone else was ever charged. <\/p>\n

And so goes the final post of 1947.
