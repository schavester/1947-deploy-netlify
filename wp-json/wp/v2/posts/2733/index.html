June 4, 1947
Downtown<\/p>\n

Gloria Hale\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s first day on the job at the Los Angeles County Employees Cafeteria ended dramatically when she got herself locked into the second floor dining room at 224 North Main Street<\/a> after closing time. She rapped on the interior door for a couple of hours before attracting attention; somebody summoned police.<\/p>\n

Unfortunately neither of the keys obtained from the County Employees offices on Maple Street fit the locks, so Sgt. Goldsberry called the hook and ladder men, who proposed an aerial escape. A crowd gathered as the ladder was extended to the Cafeteria window. But Miss Gale wouldn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t dream of descending. If she was wearing slacks the ladder would be no bother, but, really… <\/span>all those looky loos would see right up her skirt!<\/p>\n

No problem: a fireman went down rung by rung just behind her, protecting the lady\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s modesty. Miss Hale called her rescuers angels and scurried home to 953 Arapahoe Street<\/a> to sleep off her embarrassment.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
