Feb. 28, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

An old and massive California live oak used to mark the division between three Spanish land grants lies in pieces on the ground because an Edison foreman refused to run a transmission line around it. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe tree was a full hundred feet in its spread,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and stood on the end of a little plateau, all alone in its greatness. The massive trunk could not be circled by three men stretching their arms and touching their fingertips\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dhardly by four men. Above, it split into four great branches that spread out and out and then again downward, containing with an evergreen shield a refuge where two full companies of soldiers might have bivouacked in comfort.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The live oak marks the junction of the San Rafael, Los Feliz and Providencia land grants, The Times says, adding: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe tree itself stands within the Providencia but it was a starting point from which direction was taken.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe foreman is said to be in grave danger of losing his position and is very repentant,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d the paper says. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cHe bawled like a spanked bad boy before the board (note: the property was owned by the Water Board, which had given Edison permission to run the line). However the great old tree is lying among a smother of chips and there is no way to replace the work of nature\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s 300 years.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Bonus fact: The San Rafael, Providencia and San Rafael land grants<\/a> touched at a point in Burbank that is circled by the on-ramp for the southbound Golden State Freeway at the westbound lanes of Burbank Boulevard. <\/p>\n
