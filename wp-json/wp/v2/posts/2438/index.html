December 21, 1947
Hollywood<\/p>\n

Wiley and Zelda Mills, both 65, took sleeping pills in their apartment at 1753 1\/2 N. Berendo St.<\/a> after preparing their wills and writing apologetic notes. Zelda’s to their son Francis in Berkeley read ” We are sorry to have to do this now. But it is the only thing left. Dad and I talked it out and there would be no use of my trying to go on alone. We love you very much. Mother.”<\/p>\n

The couple’s son-in-law Cambern Cottrell, 1025 S. Westmoreland Ave., alerted police when he was unable to get the Mills on the phone or to answer their door. When officers L.T. Napier and J.H. Stein entered the apartment, they found Wiley dead and Zelda unconscious. She is in critical condition in General Hospital.<\/p>\n

The couple was apparently despondent over financial problems and the death of Cambern’s wife, their daughter Marjorie, from pneumonia four years ago.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
