April 24, 1907<\/div>\n
Los Angeles<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Doctor Mary Green had a thriving practice once, for which she rented an office at 624 Fifth Street; she lives in a comfortable suite of rooms in the Hotel Avalon, also on Fifth.\u00c2\u00a0 And yet the Good Doctor, who has practiced in Chicago, Cincinnati, and San Francisco before landing in Los Angeles, is said to be unable to show any certificate showing she is a graduated physician.\u00c2\u00a0 It is said as well that she is addicted to morphine.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Last night, according to the proprietor of the Avalon, a man (claiming to be her husband) visited; a short time later, Doctor Mary Green lay dying.\u00c2\u00a0 At Receiving Hospital she was indeed found to be a heavy user of the needle.\u00c2\u00a0 And, as letters from a Doctor Anderson pointed out (penned after a recent trip of Green\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s to Emergency General), she seemed likely to take her life.\u00c2\u00a0 While that life was saved at Receiving, it remains to be seen whether Doctor Mary Green will make it through much more life-time.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
