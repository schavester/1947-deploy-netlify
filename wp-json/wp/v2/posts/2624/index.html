<\/a>August 17, 1947
Long Beach<\/p>\n

With his divorce papers burning a hole in his pocket, and his heart, 30-year-old James Parker of 2246 Clifford Street stopped today at Marie Baines\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 shooting gallery at 315 W. Pike Street and laid down a 5-spot. He picked up the .22 automatic pistol that was chained to the counter and fired at four targets. The fifth bullet found a home in Parker\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s right temple. He died at Seaside Hospital at 6:15pm.<\/p>\n

When police examined the court papers found on the body, they discovered Parker\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s penciled notes denying each of his wife\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s allegations. He was next due in court at 9:30am Friday.<\/p>\n
