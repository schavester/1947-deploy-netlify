We take for granted all that easily obtainable marihuana of our teens, and that ever-so-precious peyote gobbled in our twenties, and all this easily obtainable Xanax of our thirties; back in the day, there was just booze. You didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t have to go cop, and everything you did score had rigorous quality control. Your only worry was making sure you got to Manny\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Grog n\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 Groc before the Tick Tock Lounge called last call. That, and being certain you had a little something stashed for the moaning after.
<\/p>\n

Liquor should carry a Surgeon General\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s warning that it will not<\/span> make you Fitzgerald or Miller or Hemingway. With a little luck, though, it will neither make you Myron Funk.<\/p>\n

The city has upped the numbering of Bellflower\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Burton Street from three digit to five, so it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s impossible to say which was Mrs. Lund\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s home. They likely all have booze and ironing boards, but perhaps the residents are now inside sparking bongloads and playing Illbleed<\/span>. Like, copacetic.<\/p>\n

So we\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll leave you with an image of fingersniffing, ladykilling Funk, a man to have obviously been played by John Belushi in the biopic. Belushi, the man whacked by Cathy Smith, a woman whose body Mae Lund\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s soul vengefully haunted.<\/p>\n
