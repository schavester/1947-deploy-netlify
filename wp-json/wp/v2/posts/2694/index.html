“The [Babalon] Working began in 1945-46, a few months before Crowley’s death in 1947, and just prior to the wave of unexplained aerial phenomena now recalled as the ‘Great Flying Saucer Flap’\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u00a6Parsons opened a door and something flew in.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/em><\/p>\n

-Kenneth Grant, O.T.O.<\/p>\n

Rocketry! Space craft! Unspeakable rites unleashing Neosatanic magickal Gnostic deities! Ah, Devil\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Gate, the import of your name is lost on no-one. <\/p>\n


(Taken from the historic, abandoned Flint Canyon Bridge.)<\/p>\n

Jack \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBelarion\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Parsons and pals experimented with rocketry here, in the very spot\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dunbeknownst to them\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dGoddard had. (Now JPL, aka Jack Parson’s Lab, stands a stone’s throw to the north.) Parsons lived nearby on Orange Grove\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u00a6where the cops were often called to break up parties where nude pregnant women danced in fire (and L. Ron Hubbard was shtupping everyone\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s girlfriends). <\/p>\n

The Gate is also known for multiple unsolved child disappearances. That, and the dangers of motoring near it when the blast from a solid-fuel booster (or tentacle of Cthulhu) can knock you into the Arroyo.<\/p>\n
