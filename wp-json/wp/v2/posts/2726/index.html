June 8, 1947
Corner of Bixel & 7th Streets<\/a>, Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Nice girls know the difference between a tavern and an automobile. So when Mrs. Deloris Keefer, 24, of 725 S. Bixel Street <\/a>was propositioned by a fella who suggested she stop waiting for her hubby and hop into his car for a little tipple, she said nope.<\/p>\n

The would-be barkeep was insistent, though, and grabbed and choked the lady. This so irked Deloris that she slipped off one shapely pump (a dainty size 5 \u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00bd, for those keeping score) and laid into to the stinker. He grabbed the shoe away from her, in the process dropping some identifying papers. There was no way Deloris was letting a perfectly good shoe run off with a masher, so she snatched it away as the befuddled fink took a motorized powder.<\/p>\n

Police used the ID to track down Samuel J. Blight, 22, of 122 43rd Street, Manhattan Beach<\/a>, and his pal Raymond M. Johnson, 20, of 6121 Citrus Avenue<\/a>, both of whom are sitting in County Jail on suspicion of attempted kidnapping. Deloris Keefer remained shod at press time.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
