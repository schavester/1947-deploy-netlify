Oakvid feared his child was not sane. Sometimes we see too much of ourselves in our children.<\/p>\n

Theodore Oakvid and his little Sophy:
<\/p>\n

Apparently Theo was under the thrall of Social Darwinism, but took it a little too far. Contemporary accounts note \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cthe patient asserted he sincerely believed that only the fit should live and that his daughter was among those unfit.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

Those who take their Herbert Spencer way too seriously end up in San Bernadino:

…at the Patton State Hospital.<\/p>\n

At some point the beautiful 1925 Bell High School enlarged itself to the south and a playing field ate up the east side of Flora.
<\/p>\n

But the field didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t stretch all the way down to Florence Avenue, leaving this little house intact. Imagine a collection of these running up the street.
<\/p>\n

Or perhaps the Oakvid residence looked something like its one-time \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153cross the street neighbor.
<\/p>\n
