Feb. 5, 2007
\nSierra Madre<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Because it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s celebrating its centennial this month, I paid a visit to Sierra Madre and while savoring a cinnamon dolce latte at the local Starbucks, watched the sun set on a historic Union 76 ball. A perfect fusion of two projects.<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

And here\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Sierra Madre\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Old North Church, with the artillery piece in the park across the street. Note the problem I encountered with lighting. Architectural photography is surely not my forte. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Now for the business at hand. I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ve often thought that with a century of lawmaking under its belt, the state Legislature might want to take the afternoon off. After all, with more than a century of making laws, what\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s left to regulate? <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The Times provides a tidy answer to my question. Here’s what the Legislature was wrestling with 100 years ago:<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>The Senate unanimously passes a ban on docking horses\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 tails and prohibits anyone from bringing horses with docked tails into the state. Those who own horses with docked tails would have to register them with the local county officials. <\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>The Senate passes a bill authorizing the governor to declare \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBud and Arbor Day.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>The Senate passes a bill setting dairy standards and a bill to keep the polls open from 6 a.m. to 6 p.m.<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>A committee urges the Senate to pass Sen. Black\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s tax exemption bill for all the buildings at Stanford as well as the bonds the university holds in trust. <\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>Sen. Wolfe introduces a bill making all robberies committed with a deadly weapon between sunset and sunrise punishable by death or life in prison.<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>Assemblyman Grove L. Johnson introduces a \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cno seat, no fare\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d bill providing that railroad passengers who cannot find a seat need not pay. The bill would include streetcars.<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>Assemblyman Johnson introduces a bill requiring firearms dealers to keep records of gun buyers\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 names and addresses.<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>The Assembly passes a bill by the late Assemblyman Burke making it illegal to spit on sidewalks or in trains, cars and other public conveyances.<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00b7 <\/span><\/span>Sen. Sanford introduces a bill seeking to restrict corporate donations to political campaigns. I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2m so glad the Senate wrapped that up 100 years ago so it can get on to more pressing matters. <\/p>\n
