<\/span>July 28, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Police still don’t know who did it–adulterated the city’s soya sauce supply with quantities of arsenic nearly sufficient to cause death. Dozens of Japanese residents became ill<\/a> earlier this month after ingesting rice seasoned with the poison, but all have since recovered.<\/p>\n

Health inspectors today supervised the dumping of about 1500 gallons of suspect soya sauce, tipping drums down drains at a Little Tokyo wholesale business at 114 Weller Stree<\/a>t. Another 48,500 gallons will soon face the same fate.
<\/span>
The sickly sweet odor of the sauce lingered on the air, bearing with it perhaps a hint of death, as the men from the health department acted to protect all citizens, even those of Axis descent.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
