\n
<\/a>August 18, 1947
San Bernardino<\/p>\n

After several days fruitless \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201csearching,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Robert F. Mehaffey, 37-year-old ex-con, finally stopped lying to Sheriff James Stocker when the latter told him that they were going to walk through the desert sands until they found Lewis D. Edds\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 body.<\/p>\n

Edds was the 65-year-old amateur prospector who vanished in April while on a vacation from his work as a steel plant guard. He was last seen in the company of a younger man. War Bonds belonging to Edds were later tracked from San Francisco to Texas and thence to Mehaffey, a hitchhiker whom the elderly man had befriended.<\/p>\n

Following the Sheriff\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s threat, Mehaffey replied \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThat won\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t be necessary\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and led him straight to a set of dessicated remains, located stuffed in a drain pipe just off the Lancaster-Palmdale Highway, two miles south of Victorville<\/a>. This was 55 miles from the last spot Mehaffey had suggested.<\/p>\n

Pending examination of dental records, the Sheriff assumes they\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ve found the missing Mr Edds.<\/p>\n
