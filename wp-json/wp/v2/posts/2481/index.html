November 30, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Hurrah for the telephone!<\/p>\n

First, Mrs. L.B. Beddoe, 4587 Date Ave., La Mesa, received a call from daughter Pamela Evans, who said she was going to kill herself. Mama called the LAPD business office and asked if someone could please stop her.<\/p>\n

Radio patrolmen J.P. Hooper and T.A. Gibson raced to Pamela’s pad at 104 N. Catalina<\/a>, where the 19-year-old department store worker was passed out beside an empty pill box. The officers rushed her to Hollywood Receiving Hospital for a stomach pumping. Pamela presently revived, and murmured of the financial woes that had inspired her act.<\/p>\n

But happily the phone had still been working, and so the lady lives. Moral: always pay your phone bill first.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
