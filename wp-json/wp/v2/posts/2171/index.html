November 17, 1927
Los Angeles

Charley Chase<\/a> received a sentence of fifty days—suspended—from Judge Baird today, for while Chase admitted to taking a sip of whisky before crashing his auto into the back of a taxicab on Hollywood Boulevard last Monday morning, the magistrate judged Charley to be only reckless, not drunk.<\/p>\n

Chase is today best known for his work in promoting the exclamation-mark’d picture.  Long before 1947, the year which saw two noir exclamation-mark’d masterpieces—Railroaded!<\/em> and Boomerang!<\/em>—and long before little girls screamed Them!<\/em> and everyone shouted Oklahoma!<\/em> and then we all yelled Faster, Pussycat! Kill! Kill!<\/em>, Chase starred in Nurse to You!<\/em>, Okay Toots!<\/em>, You Said a Hatful!<\/em>, What a Bozo!<\/em>, Skip the Maloo!<\/em>, and of course ¡Huye, Faldas!<\/em>, to name but a few.  He also asked the cinematic questions Are Brunettes Safe?<\/em> and Is Everybody Happy?<\/em> and Isn’t Life Terrible?<\/em> and What Price Goofy?<\/em> and Is Marriage the Bunk?<\/em> and Should Husbands Be Watched?<\/em> and Why Go Home?<\/em> and while these aren’t exactly What<\/em> Ever Happened to Baby Jane?<\/em> or Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf?<\/em> (or What’s the Matter With Helen?<\/em> or Who Ever Slew Auntie Roo?<\/em> for that matter) they sure beat the stuffing out of Where’s Poppa?<\/em> and What’s So Bad About Feeling Good?<\/em> <\/p>\n

Anyway.  The judge should have thrown the book at Chase for his whisky-sippin’, because his alcoholism killed him at the age of 47, in 1940.  But then, what was Judge Baird to do?  Send Chase to meetings?  Bill Wilson<\/a> wouldn’t get hot flashes for another seven years.<\/p>\n
