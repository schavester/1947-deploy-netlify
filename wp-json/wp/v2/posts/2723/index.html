June 10, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Superior Court Judge Charles W. Fricke today denied confessed killer Gerald Snow Welch<\/a>, 19, his fondest wish, and sentenced him to life in prison for the April 19 killing of Delores Fewkes<\/a>, a student at Montebello High School who family members say had broken up with Welch repeatedly due to his peculiar philosophy, Further, she did not believe in suicide.<\/p>\n

According to the depressive Welch, Fewkes said she couldn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t live without him; they had attempted suicide together on two occasions prior to their April assignation at the Horse Flats picnic grounds in Angeles National Forest<\/a>. She broke a date with another boy to join Welch, who told her he had a surprise for her. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThis is the time,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d he said. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cIt\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s all right with me,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d she supposedly replied. So he waited until morning, when it was warmer and his hands stopped shaking, and botched the murder-suicide, later carrying the girl\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s body down the mountain to police.<\/p>\n

After the verdict, which he described as a \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cdirty trick,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Welch told the court that he would promptly rectify its failure to sentence him to death by killing himself, so he and Dolores could be happy together.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
