July 2, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Madge Meredith<\/a>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dwhat went wrong? The little Iowa City blonde, discovered while working as a cashier in the 20th Century-Fox commissary, later signed to RKO where she starred in \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cChild of Divorce\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe Falcon\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Adventure,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d though her contract recently lapsed, is today a fugitive, wanted on kidnap charges!<\/p>\n

Two men are in custody following the daring escape from remote Lopez Canyon<\/a> of Miss Meredith\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s former business manager, 38-year-old Nicholas Dan Gianaclis and his assistant Verne Vinson Davis.<\/p>\n

The men were allegedly shanghaid when they arrived at the base of Laurel Canyon Boulevard for a planned meeting with Miss Meredith. She turned up in a new red convertible, and motioned for them to follow her to Gianaclis’ house up the hill. Near it, she used her car to block Gianaclis\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 car while pointing him out to three associates in a third vehicle, one of whom administered a blackjack beating while the others held guns. Gianaclis and Davis were forced into their assailants\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 car and driven for more than an hour, with blows punctuating every move they made. On arrival in Lopez Canyon, they were held at gunpoint for six hours, until they managed to escape and find aid at Slocum Ranch.<\/p>\n

What\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s it all about, Madgey? The lady recently made noise about suing her former manager for substituting a grant deed for a mortgage paper on the house at 8444 Magnolia Drive<\/a> where until recently she and her family lived, and where the two hostages now reside, though after some initial testimony, the matter was dropped. Gianaclis identified one of his attackers as William Klinkenburg, 32-year-old cook, 6439 Agnes Street. When arrested, Klinkenburg was holding a gun belonging to Barclay Leon Thomas, 33, of 6936 Woody Trail. Barclay\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s three-year marriage to Gianaclis\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 daughter was annulled last week. Thomas denies any knowledge of the kidnapping and assault.<\/p>\n

Madge Meredith\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s mother, Mrs. Laura Massow, reached at her home at 8942 West 24th Street, said she had no idea where her daughter, who came home only occasionally, might be.<\/p>\n

Police are still looking for the actress, and two mysterious men named \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cJim\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBill,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and trying to determine motive for the strange incident.<\/p>\n

[usually at 1947project we leave our cases frozen in time, but this one is just too juicy not to share further reference material<\/a>.
