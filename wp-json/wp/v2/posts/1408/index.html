\nFeb.13, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

An enormous masked ball for the city\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s elite was staged on Mardi Gras at Kramer\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s Studio and Dancing Academy, 1500 S. Figueroa<\/a>.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Kramer’s Hall, as it was informally known.<\/span><\/div>\n

The Times, in a rare bylined article\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dby Katherine Thompson\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dgives
\nan exhaustive account of decorations and costumes. Rather than list all the women\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s outfits, I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll only comment on them: Spanish senoritas, flower girls, cowboys and a couple of ladies dressed as Chinese girls, which seems a peculiar choice given the attitude toward the Chinese in Los Angeles at the time.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

One woman dressed as \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cMy Lady Nicotine\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d her gown decorated with what The Times estimated, perhaps in exaggeration, as a thousand cigar bands. Several others were dressed as \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cNight.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Costumes for the men included cowboys or vaqueros, a Spanish grandee, a French pastry cook and a cardinal.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

The other celebration underway was the first anniversary of the Hotel Alexandria, which marked the occasion with a massive fireworks display.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cAt 8 o\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2clock last night, several thousand dollars worth of fireworks were set off from the roof of the Alexandria,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cOne particularly attractive piece pictured the great lobby of the building. During the evening a Hungarian quartette furnished music.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n
