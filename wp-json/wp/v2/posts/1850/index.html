<\/a>Jan. 23, 1907
\nLos Angeles <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Pity, for a moment, Felix Chavarino, caught in the grips, not of opium, morphine or heroin, but of citrus, for he is a \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201clemon fiend.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

He was arrested after begging for food in a small restaurant. Chavarino didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t want anything else on the menu, pleading for a \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cle-mon,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d a \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cle-mon.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/span><\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cGaunt, unkempt and weird looking, he crouched there, disdaining all offers,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cWith skin turned to the yellow hue of the lemon, lips drawn back sore and red, from protruding teeth, skin stretched tightly over cheekbones; matted hair, through which his fingers constantly moved; eyes bulging and glassy, he was a living picture of the effects of excessive use of the lemon.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Someone gave him money to buy food, but took it away when Chavarino set off for the nearest fruit stand. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Chavarino, who had already served months in jail, was convicted of vagrancy and given 30 days. There is no further record of him in The Times, so we don\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t know what became of him.<\/p>\n
