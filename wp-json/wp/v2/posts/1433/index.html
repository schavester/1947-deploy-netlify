Jan. 30, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Recent rains have left the city\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s streets in terrible shape, as The Times shows in a photo taken at 1st<\/sup> Street and Spring. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

This wagon, pulled by a strong team, plunged up to its hubs in one of the potholes and the horses were unable to free it. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cUnder the whip and vociferous admonitions of their driver, they were helpless to pull it out from the stinking muck in which, hub deep, it stood,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The driver abandoned the wagon, The Times says, posing an obstacle to other traffic. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cStreet conditions in Los Angeles never were so bad as they are today under our new-fangled, high-salaried and pompous Board of Public Works. <\/span>When we had a street superintendent it was sometimes possible to \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153get a move on,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says.<\/p>\n
