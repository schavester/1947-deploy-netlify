<\/p>\n

For whatever reason, Little Joe\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s is one of this author\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s favorite buildings in Los Angeles. There\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s something about that 60s Mansard\/Spanish lamp\/engaged arch thing\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u00a6so now, I digress, because I can.<\/p>\n

Little Joe\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s grocery opened at 5th and Hewitt in 1910, moving to this location in 1923, adding the restaurant in \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u015327. The neighborhood was 110% Itay at the time, \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153til Union Station displaced Chinatown and the City concocted a new one here (note the curved rooflines behind LJ’s giant backlit signage). The Nuccio family, the waitresses in red, white and green outfits, the sawdust on the floor, the Piedmonte food, hung on til 1998.<\/p>\n

Take a look while you can, folks; Little Joe\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s is being demolished by the city, in cahoots with developer Larry Bond, to become a parking structure<\/a> serving the forthcoming Chinesque \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBlossom Plaza\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d mall. <\/p>\n

(Behind Little Joe\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s is the 1831 Capitol Milling complex, slated for major additions in its morph into yet another mixed-use behemoth.
