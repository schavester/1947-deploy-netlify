May 31, 1947
Pasadena<\/p>\n

Mrs. Vida Dell, employed by Ives & Warren Mortuary at 100 N. Hill Ave<\/a>., is used to dealing with the dead and the bereaved. But she wasn’t prepared for today’s frugal visitor, Joseph Arthur Rawles, who apparently saw no point in incuring the cost of two rides in a hearse.<\/p>\n

The 70-year-old jewelry store worker, despondent over poor health and fading eyesight, entered the mortuary, tapped on the office glass to get Mrs. Dell’s attention, then drank from a bottle of poison and collapsed. A note on his body explained Rawles’ reasons. The deceased lived at 650. N. Madison Ave<\/a>.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
