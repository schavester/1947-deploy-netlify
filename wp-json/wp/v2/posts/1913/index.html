April 19, 1927
Pasadena <\/p>\n

Yee-ouch!<\/em> Mrs. Fred Anderson, Pasadena matron of 1304 North Catalina Ave.<\/a>, was dressing this morning when she slid one shapely foot into a stocking she’d last worn several months back on a visit to the remote  Imperial Valley<\/a>. <\/p>\n

It seems she picked up a hitchhiker on that trip, for inside the toe wriggled one very annoyed small scorpion. It struck the lady thrice, and off she hopped to Emergency Hospital, where Chief Surgeon Clement C. Troensegaard told her how lucky she was to have been foot-stung and not tongue-stung, for only on especially tender spots are scorpion wounds fatal. <\/p>\n

Still, we bet this is one gal who will never put on a pair of hose again without checking them very, very carefully for unwanted guests…. as should we all.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
