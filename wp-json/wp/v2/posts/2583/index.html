September 14, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Those are wedding bells you’re hearing for hard-boiled pen-man James M. Cain, who took the occasion of the granting of Aileen Pringle’s divorce petition to announce that he intends to wed singer Florence MacBeth in October. The bride, widow of Captain Edward Whitwell R.A.F., has had a succesful stage career with the Chicago Grand Opera, despite sporting so dangerous a name. Post-nuptials, the lovebirds are off to Louisiana, where Cain intends to research a civil war novel while Flo begins her memoirs.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
