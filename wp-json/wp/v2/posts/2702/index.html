June 22, 1947
Glendale<\/p>\n

Swans mate for life, and no amount of publicity-seeking, Trans-American hanky panky can change that simple and profound truism.<\/p>\n

You\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll perhaps remember the events of April 17, when Gus the Swan, resident of The Pond, Forest Lawn Memorial Park<\/a>, and recently left bereft by the death of his beloved Elvira, was flown (via American, actually) to Egypt, Mass. to select a new bride from among the purebred flock at Charles P. Chase\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s swan ranch. The droopy bird, nicknamed Gloomy Gus for the obvious suffering in his gait, was put into a cage with four lady swans, a quadrifoil reminder that Elvira was no more. Perhaps to put an end to the obscene display, after six days he made a selection. Henrietta was her name, and the two titular lovebirds flew, or rather were flown, back to the boneyard to honeymoon.<\/p>\n

Despite news stories trumpeting (sorry) Gus\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 new joy, inside it seems he still grieved. Early this morning his body was found deep among the reeds of his pond. Henrietta is inconsolable. And the men whose business it is to bury the dead in a timely and moderately tasteful fashion seem a tad unsure of what to do next. Hold private rites, and then bury Gus next to Elvira, they reckon.<\/p>\n
