September 24, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

John Donald La Chance and his young family have been evicted from their Quonset hut home in Rodger Young Village<\/a>. The 32-year-old former marine private claims his rent has gone unpaid because his $60 monthly disability check for September hasn’t arrived.<\/p>\n

But Ed Leibeck of the City Housing Authority counters that La Chance has failed to pay his rent repeatedly, and would have been bounced in May had the VA not stepped in to help. He has also received aid from Public Assistance. Now, since La Chance refuses to take a test to see if he is able to work, and the rent is three weeks late, La Chance, wife Ruth, and little Barbara Ann and baby Donald Lee have been removed from hut #1038.<\/p>\n

La Chance enlisted in June 1942 and was discharged 14 months later, after suffering an injury at Camp Pendleton.<\/p>\n

Further reading on the ex-servicemen’s community of Rodger Young Village is in The Provisional City: Los Angeles Stories of Architecture and Urbanism<\/b><\/a>; see also these message board posts<\/a>
