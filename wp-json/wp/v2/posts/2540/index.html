October 17, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Sitting in Edward R. Brand’s Superior Courtroom, movie actor\/ boilermaker Gerald D. O’Neill, 50, heard his sentence of 10 to 20 years read.<\/p>\n

There was no wife there to comfort him–not Mrs. Stella Frank O’Neill (720 S. Bonnie Brae, wedding date 2\/7\/42), nor any of his subsequent bigamous brides: Mrs. Margaret Beeler Williams (156 Coolidge Ave.), Mrs. Julia Twitchell (826 W. 49th St), Mrs. Anna Gwendolyn Ashley (221. Columbia Street).<\/p>\n

Even his special lady friend and financier of his legal expenses, Mrs. Myrtle Riley (4532 Willowbrook Ave., “the sweetest girl in all the world”), sat out the special day.<\/p>\n

Attorney Richard Erwin intends to appeal. As for O’Neill, let’s hope he stays out of the dance halls, which it seems he can’t visit without proposing marriage.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
