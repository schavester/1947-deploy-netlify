Feb. 21, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Someone who hates animals is at work in Angeleno Heights, having poisoned 10 valuable dogs and several cats, The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The killer has an eye for purebreds, the paper says, avoiding mutts and mongrels. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cDetective George Home of 901 Carrillo <\/span>St. lost a fine bulldog and Patrolman C.L. Johnson of Bellevue Avenue lost a trained birddog which he had for 15 years,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The killer\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s methods remain a mystery. Many of the poisoned animals were never allowed out of the yard and one Saint Bernard was always kept chained up, the newspaper says. <\/p>\n
