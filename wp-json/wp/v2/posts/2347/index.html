February 7, 1947
West Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Harry Crocker will be making his own breakfast if his neighbors on N. Westbourne Drive<\/a> have anything to do with it. Six of them have successfully sued to have Mrs. Isabel Crocker and daughters Alicia, Jean and Muriel evicted on the grounds that the mother is 3\/4 Indian and the girls half so afflicted.<\/p>\n

Although Superior Judge Ruben S. Schmidt ruled Thursday that the women must leave their home, in a neighborhood where residence is restricted to Caucasians, the distaff Crockers vow to fight all the way to the Supreme Court, if necessary. Schmidt did grant the family thirty days in which to secure new dwellings for the those Crockers of mixed blood, noting that the Mister, a film cameraman, was welcome to remain at number 435 alone.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
