April 4, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

On March 19, after his third grade classes at Allesandro School, 9-year-old Murl Duncan Cooke, Jr. failed to return to the home he shared at 2910 Gilroy Avenue with brother Newton, 10, and their widowed mother Pearl.<\/p>\n

Since then, the boy has been sleeping rough, eating whatever people gave him. Juvenile Officers W.B. Smith and J.W. Scheck finally spotted the kid huddling in a liquor store doorway near where Beverly meets Vermont<\/a> last night, and brought him into Georgia Street juvenile division for a big slice of banana cream pie and a warm cot.<\/p>\n

Why was little Murl alone on the street? The next morning, he told officers, “I ran away once before. I didn’t go home till after 8 o’clock and I got a whippin’ for it. That’s why I didn’t want to go home this time.” He was returned to his mother’s care.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
