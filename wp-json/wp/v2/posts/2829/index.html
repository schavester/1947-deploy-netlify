April 8, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Robert Ahlberg, 21, late of Denver, was happy to see police officers M. C. Jacobsen and L.O. Sheets early today, even as they handcuffed him.<\/p>\n

Alerted by his victim’s cries, two students in a nearby hotel saw Ahlberg attacking a 46-year-old woman in City Library Park (Fifth and Hope Streets<\/a>) and dragging her 75 feet into the bushes. A crowd several dozen stong gathered at the scene, but did not rescue the woman, telling arriving officers they were afraid her attacker might have a gun.<\/p>\n

Officers Jacobsen and Sheets dragged Ahlberg from the bushes, divesting him of a knife with which he had threatened his victim. At this point, the crowd became brave, and cried out “Let us have him!”<\/p>\n

Ahlberg was booked into Central Jail, where he gave his local address as 621 1\/2 W. Sixth Street<\/a>, just a short stroll from the assault scene.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
