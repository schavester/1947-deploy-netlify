June 2, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Congenital insanity compounded by war jitters is the desperate claim of Erwin M. Walker, 29, confessed slayer of California Highway Patrolman Loren C. Roosevelt on June 5 of last year. Roosevelt was fatally shot when he approached Walker, who was casing a market at Los Feliz Blvd. and Brunswick Ave.,<\/a> and asked for identification; Walker also admitted to wounding Det. Lt. Colin C. Forbes last April 25, when Forbes sought to arrest Walker, a pre-war civilian employee of the Glendale Police Department, on a charge of seeking to unload $40,000 in \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201chot\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d motion-picture equipment to Willard Starr, sound engineer of 1347 Fifth Ave<\/a>. Starr had called police to stake out salesman \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cPaul C. Norris\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d when he came by with the goods.<\/p>\n

All true, says Erwin, but wait\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dthere are mitigating circumstances. Like dear old grandmama on dad\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s side, a mental patient for these last 32 years, her case described by Erwin\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s father Weston, a County Flood Control worker residing at 1013 Cordova St., Glendale<\/a>. Or the half dozen other nuts on the family tree. As for Erwin, so what that three psychiatrists say he\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s sane? The family knows otherwise. He\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s been hinky ever since coming home from the South Pacific. Mrs. Irene L. Walker, Erwin\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s mother, contrasted the affectionate boy she turned over to Uncle Sam with the weird loner who returned.<\/p>\n

Erwin himself described his guilt over his best friend\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s bayoneting on Leyte Island<\/a>, an attack he believed might have been averted had he given an order to dig foxholes. His colleagues agreed, and shunned him thereafter.<\/p>\n

Erwin was finally arrested December 20 at his apartment at 1831 \u00c3\u201a\u00c2\u00bd N. Argyle Ave<\/a>. after a gun battle with detectives who surprised the sleeping ex-GI as he cradled a sub-machine gun and .45 caliber automatic. They shot him a couple of times. At the hospital, he was found to have old bullet wounds, a souvenir of the April battle with Forbes\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 partner, Sgt. S, W. Johnson. These Erwin said he had treated himself.<\/p>\n

After returning from service, Erwin refused to return to his dispatcher\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s gig at the Glendale P.D., citing the lousy pay scale. Instead, it is alleged, he entered into a career of robbery, safecracking and hold ups, obtaining approximately $70,000 in these fields until the time of his arrest.<\/p>\n

In addition to his daffy relatives, the enterprising Erwin is the nephew of former Deputy District Attorney Herbert Walker.<\/p>\n

The case inspired an acclaimed noir film starring Richard Basehart.<\/p>\n
