May 25, 1927
Venice<\/p>\n

Seems a traveling sewing machine salesman dropped by Mrs. L.K. Sitton’s home at 1004 Electric Avenue and, when she complained of a slight headache, mentioned that he was an expert in Swedish massage and offered to relieve her. <\/p>\n

The Times reported that "He fled… when his victim screamed and her husband returned unexpectedly." Though we wonder if perhaps things didn’t happen quite<\/em> in that order.<\/p>\n

In any event, Robert S. Harrell, 41, was arrested at San Juan and Sixth Avenue<\/a> on charges of attempting an attack on the lady. <\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
