June 30, 1947<\/div>\n

San Fernando Valley<\/p>\n

Film costumer Vera West<\/a> died in her swimming pool at 5119 Bluebell Ave.<\/a> over the weekend. She suffered from marital difficulties, but the blackmail to which she alluded in her suicide note was, according to husband Jack C. West, a figment of her imagination. Mr. West claims he was staying at the Beverly Hills Hotel when his wife took her unfortunate dip, following a bad argument, and in anticipation of Mrs. West\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s consultation with a divorce attorney.<\/p><\/div>\n
\n
Assistant county autopsy surgeon Dr. Marvin Goodwin\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s initial report was of asphyxia, probably due to drowning, but Dr. Frederick Newbarr, his superior, is refusing to sign a death certificate until additional tests are performed.<\/div>\n
