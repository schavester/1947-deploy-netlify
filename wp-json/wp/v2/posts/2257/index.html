February 6, 1927<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Over the weekend, the District Attorney’s crackerjack Prohibition task force proved beyond the shadow of a doubt their devotion to the cause.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Then again, after the theatrical busts they staged, it’s also possible that rum squad head George Contreras and his men simply craved adventure, danger, or an excuse to wear women’s clothing.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
The fun started when Contreras and two of his agents entered a home while dressed as telephone repairmen.\u00c2\u00a0 When they were unable to find the hooch inside, they flung open the chicken coop in the backyard, and were greeted by three full-grown lions.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Yes, three full-grown lions.\u00c2\u00a0 In a chicken coop.<\/em><\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
All three men promptly vaulted over the fence, and sought shelter across the street.\u00c2\u00a0 Here, they discovered A. Hernandez’s 25 gallon still, and arrested him.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
The lions were pets, they later discovered, though “not particularly fond of strangers.”<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
After the lion incident, Contreras dressed two more of his agents up in women’s clothing and took them joy-riding to 217 E. 61st St<\/a>..\u00c2\u00a0 Here, he pulled up to the home of a suspected moonshiner, Raymond Manley, and asked for “some liquor for the girlfriends.”\u00c2\u00a0 When Manley brought out a bottle, police raided the place and discovered an enormous still, 180 gallons of whiskey, and 39 barrels of mash.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
So, to sum up:\u00c2\u00a0 a man crafts a tasty beverage by hand, and goes to jail for his trouble, while the man who raises adult lions, in a chicken coop, no less, walks free.\u00c2\u00a0 And our rum squad seems to enjoy playing dress-up a little more than the average adult probably should.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Up is down, left is right.\u00c2\u00a0 Sheesh.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
