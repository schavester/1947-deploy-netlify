Feb. 23, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The Scotch Thistles beat the English Victorias 3-2 in Southern California\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s first soccer game since the Caledonians and the Pasadena team met at Agricultural Park in 1890, The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Despite the poor condition of the field, the players displayed excellent skill and teamwork. The Times said the teams were only formed three months before and <\/span>notes that more teams were expected next year.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cAs a good, healthful sport, requiring both athletic training and skill, \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153soccer\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 seems to have a place to fill here as elsewhere. Apparently, however, it is even less qualified than rugby to take the place of American football,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. <\/p>\n
