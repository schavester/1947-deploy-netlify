March 30, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Career footpad Ray Mathews, 28, 417 S. Boylston St.<\/a>, made the mistake of plying his trade too close to home, when he was observed early today riffling the purse of his across the hall neighbor, Mrs. Harry Schwerberg.<\/p>\n

The lady yelled, which alerted another neighbor, Kenneth Craig, who shot Mathews as he tried to flee the building. Mathews then returned to his own apartment, leaving again by way of a ladder out the window. Responding officers found the wounded man hiding in a hedge, and conveyed him to the Georgia Street Receiving Hospital, where he was booked on suspicion of robberies in San Francisco. Also taken into custody was a .45 caliber revolver, allegedly tossed aside by Mathews.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
