June 17, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Joe (or more like Jane) Citizen of the 700 block of S. New Hampshire Ave.<\/a> just couldn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t stand the sight of the little blonde girl and her dog asleep in the back seat of that car one more night, so on the third incidence she phoned police. Sure enough, radio officers Clyde Giroux and D.R. Lynch discovered 7-year-old Linda Henderson and her dog Butch snoozing in the back of a car parked in front of number 747.<\/p>\n

Asked where she lived, the yawning gamine explained \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cwhen the sun comes up my mamma comes and takes me to a caf\u00c3\u0192\u00c2\u00a9, but I can\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t tell you where I live for we\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2re looking for a place.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d But mamma was nowhere to be seen this evening, so officers left a note on the car. Then Butch and Linda were processed at Wilshire Police Station and the child sent along to Juvenile Hall.<\/p>\n

A friend told Linda\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s mamma what had happened, and Mrs. Louise M. Carringer (nee Henderson, 36, divorced) appeared, explaining that during her search for housing she only occasionally left Linda alone. Dep. City Attorney Perry Thomas responded by sending mamma, charged with child neglect, over to spend the night in the Lincoln Heights Jail.<\/p>\n

Of Butch, who apparently made no move to stop the strange men from removing his charge from the vehicle, we know no more.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
