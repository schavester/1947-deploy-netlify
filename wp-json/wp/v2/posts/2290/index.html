\nMarch 17, 1927
\nBakersfield\n<\/p>\n

\nFrustrated at being rejected for employment as a nurse by Kern Hospital, twenty-one year old Gladys Maryon Lindley came up with a plan \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u0153 become a man!\n<\/p>\n

\nDressed in men\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s clothing and answering to the name \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBilly\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d, Gladys presented herself at Kern Hospital once again. Instead of seeking employment as a nurse, \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBilly\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d applied for the job of a male orderly this time and was hired on the spot.\n<\/p>\n

\nGladys went undetected until three months later, when the secret of her identity was revealed by one of her former teachers. Recognizing \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cBilly\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d at the hospital, the teacher ratted her out to hospital administrators. Gladys – aka Billy – resigned immediately.\n<\/p>\n

\nPerhaps naively, the LA Times reported that \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201ca real desire to do hospital work was given as the explanation of the masquerade\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d.\n<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
