June 19, 1947
Pasadena<\/p>\n

Frustrated by her husband\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s refusal to return to their home, Mrs. Jack Scher had an inspired brainstorm\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dshe\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d print up some handbills pleading her case, and hire a couple of strapping lads to distribute them in front of Scher\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s fruit stand at 170 S. Marengo.<\/a><\/p>\n

Were you among Scher\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s customers or passersby today, you might have been handed a paper which read: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cMrs. Jack Scher would like her husband, Mr. Jack Scher, of the fruit and vegetable department of the Wonder Shipping Center, to come home to his wife and child.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

The scheme backfired when Scher became incensed, and shot at one of the youths with his .22, nicking the clothing of John Brangard, 127 N. Mentor Ave. His roommate Elrod Swanson was then swatted on his shoulder with Scher\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s rifle stock. Pasadena police arrived and booked Scher, 43, for investigation of assault with a deadly weapon, but a sympathetic judge freed the man. Officers hope to get a complaint.<\/p>\n

Mrs. Scher reports that it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s now ten days since she and their ten-year-old son were abandoned at their home, 3453 Milton St., East Pasadena<\/a>.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
