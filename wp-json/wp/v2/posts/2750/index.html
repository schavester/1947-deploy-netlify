May 25, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Next time his brother Lawrence asks if he can put a safe in his bedroom closet and fly out periodically on his private plane to rummage around in it, Archie Bardin will probably say \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cnix.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Archie and the missus came home from a day at the beach to find their house at 8655 Airdrome Ave<\/a>. filled with smoke, a souvenir from the acetylene torch burglars had used to gain access to the floor safe after climbing through a bathroom window.<\/p>\n

Detective Harold B. Williams asked about the safe\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s contents, only to learn Archie had no clue. Whatever rested inside\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dcash and securities, Archie thought– was the property of brother Larry, general manager of the Indianapolis Brewing Company<\/a>. A call to Indianapolis revealed the missing cabbage to number 250,000 leaves.<\/p>\n

The missus told Detective Williams about two strange phone calls received yesterday, from an anonymous, accented caller, but otherwise clues were scarce.
