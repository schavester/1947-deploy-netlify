July 25, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Take one blonde “freelance actress” (Mildred Jenkins, aka The Bride)
Add one Alameda County rancher (A. Q. Bonnet, Jr., The Groom)
Marry them in Las Vegas<\/p>\n

<\/a>Immediately after their wedding breakfast, have the groom take the bride and her female roommate to a gambling house, where he loses all his money and repeatedly demands that his new missus stake him, because what\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s hers is his now. When she refuses, have him tell her that she takes the marriage too seriously, and that to him it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s just a good gag. Then have him drive back to the bride\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s apartment at 145 S. Reeves Drive, Beverly Hills<\/a>, where he leaves Mrs. Bonnet and her roommate, Jane Adrian, never to return.<\/p>\n

Superior Court Judge J.A. Smith said that was as a fine example of an annullable marriage as he’d lately heard, and granted the request.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
