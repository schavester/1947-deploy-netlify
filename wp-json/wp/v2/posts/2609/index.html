August 27, 1947
Arcadia<\/p>\n

She\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s home!<\/p>\n

Ruth Alice McLaren, 15-year-old runaway, has turned up in Ann Arbor, Michigan after her overly generous tips to a beautician raised the snipper\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s suspicions. Now her mother is heading East to reclaim the young lady from the local juvenile hall, while Ruth\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s invalid father Russell steams at home at 23 Fano Street<\/a>.<\/p>\n

What of the $2400 in cash (meant for the girl\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s college education) that she pinched from the fruit jar last time she left the house? While the girl initially told officers she was a war widow on the run from her in-laws, she quickly \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153fessed up. Seems she met a man in a Pasadena movie theater, and agreed to pay both their ways to Ann Arbor, where her friend said he planned to enroll at the University.<\/p>\n

Ruth was a big spender\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dbut mainly on herself. In addition to visiting the beauty parlor, she bought a fur coat, flashy duds and a radio. So who can blame her pal for taking a powder with $1000 of the fruit jar bounty soon after they reached the Mitten State?<\/p>\n

But there\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s $402 left in the kitty. Which should be just about enough, after travel expenses, to pay Ruth\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s way through beauty school, where if she\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s very lucky she\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll run into tippers as big as herself.<\/p>\n
