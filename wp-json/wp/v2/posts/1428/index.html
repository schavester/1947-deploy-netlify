Feb. 2, 1907
\nLos Angeles<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Conditions at Chutes Park are so bad that the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals is making a second inspection to see whether operator J.B. Lehigh has made any improvements before his Feb. 20 trial on charges of abuse and neglect. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Chutes is nothing more than a mud-filled stockyards of suffering animals, The Times says. <\/span>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153park\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 is a long puddle of filth, reeking with slime and mud. In the pen where three little does are confined, one of them so emaciated that it is literally hidebound, a thick green scum has formed over the stagnant pool of slime that occupies a good share of the particular part of the \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00cb\u0153park\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 where these poor little animals are shut up.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

In answer to the question of what became of the birds, an attendant replied: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cDead.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThey used to keep \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2em in cages over by the entrance, but a few weeks ago they moved \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2em all down [illegible] with the cockatoos and parrots and you can guess what happened. [Those?] great big birds just killed all the [illegible] off\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dthat\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s where the birds have gone to.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cA patient zebra paced up and down the narrow path at one edge of his cage, which was the only dry spot in the pen, and a big, beautiful elk beat his horns helplessly against the bars of his small quarters. His coat was matted with filth and the mud was a foot deep in his pen,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

Lehigh was found not guilty animal cruelty after witnesses testified that elk like to wallow in mud. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cAt times, it is in a manner necessary to their comfort,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cElk, said one old-timer whose beard hung to his waist, like to wallow and it does them good.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n
