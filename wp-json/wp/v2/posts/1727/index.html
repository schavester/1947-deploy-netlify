Jan. 15, 2007<\/p>\n

Los Angeles <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

While making my daily check of EBay<\/a>, I found an envelope from 1907 that was an interesting sequel to the “Mystery of the Burmese Fortunetellers<\/a>.” This envelope was addressed to A. Victor Segno, 701 N. Belmont. <\/p>\n

A brief check of Proquest reveals\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dwhat\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s this? A major scam artist, self-help author and wife-stealer.<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

A. Victor Segno turns out to be the operator of the American Institute of Mentalism. Here\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s how it works: Members agree to send Segno $1 ($21.30 USD 2005) a month. In return he sends out a \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201csuccess wave\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d twice a day. <\/p>\n

According to Segno\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s literature: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe vibrations which Prof. Segno is able to produce in people, through being in harmony with their mentalism, is often felt by them, though thousands of miles distant, as a sensation similar to a slight electric shock.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

And a testimonial: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cWhen I commenced taking the treatments with your club I was full of doubts as to the effects, but as I was anxious to be successful and had little to lose and all to gain I continued to take them. I have been a member less than two months and the following are the results:<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cAt the time I joined the club I was sick, but compelled to work and for very small wages. Shortly after I began to feel better. On the 7th<\/sup> of this month my employer sold me his stock of goods on credit. By the 21st<\/sup> I had paid $100 on the cost of the stock and on the 24th<\/sup> I sold the stock for ($300 or $800) over the cost and reserved an interest in the business.<\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cYou no doubt will be glad to learn that since joining your club I have improved in health, supported myself and little baby girl and made over $1,000, and risen from a servant to be a proprietor. I have also secured a position as traveling agent for a Chicago firm at a large salary. It is wonderful to me.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

Busy though he was sending out success waves, Segno was able to write two books: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cHow to Live 100 Years\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cHow to Be Happy Though Married,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d available from the institute for $3 each. Later works included \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cPersonal Magnetism,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/span>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThe Law of Mentalism<\/a>\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cHow to Have Beautiful Hair.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

Apparently Segno did a thriving business because in a few years he was able to plan a large estate at Belmont and Kane, which was featured in The Times. The letter, addressed to 701 N. Belmont, was presumable sent to the institution on \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cInspiration Point\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d over Echo Park, although I can\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t locate it now. <\/p>\n

In 1911, however, Segno left Los Angeles, ostensibly to set up a similar school in Russia. Shortly thereafter, his longtime personal secretary, Mrs. Irene Weitzel, a recently married woman whom he had employed since she was a young girl, vanished on an alleged trip to Chicago to visit her parents. <\/p>\n

In response to reporters\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 questions about whether Segno had run off with his secretary, his wife, Annie Dell Segno, <\/span>replied: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cIt isn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t true, unless my husband has lost his senses.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cAn official of the school admitted that Mrs. Segno is greatly perturbed and admitted that there had been talk about Segno and the girl for some months,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d The Times says. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cShe said that when such stories came to her ears she had hotly denied them because Segno\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s teaching and life stood out in her mind as everything ideal.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

A. Victor Segno and “success waves” in action, from one of his books. Note the beautiful hair. <\/span>\n<\/p>\n

Divorce eventually followed as Segno set up an identical scheme in Berlin. He returned to the United States about 1915 as the clouds of World War I formed over Europe. Thereafter, Segno vanished from the news while his ex-wife was mentioned in a 1923 story because she had married Harry T. Robinson, apparently a member of a robbery gang. <\/p>\n

Thanks, EBay! <\/p>\n
