October 15, 1927
Los Angeles <\/p>\n

When you can’t legally purchase a fifth of Jack Daniels<\/a>, what can you do to get a buzz and have a little fun? Well, if you are Mr. Raymond Rice, 40, of 1935 Orchard Avenue<\/a>, you will get higher than a kite by guzzling significant quantities of products loaded with ether<\/a> such as hair tonic, shellac, and canned heat, and then you’ll go for a drive. Maybe the next time Raymond gets hammered on ether he’ll stay at home. Officers Meyers and McClellan spotted him blocking traffic with his automobile and cited him for being intoxicated. <\/p>\n

Forty-five years from now gonzo<\/a> journalist Hunter S. Thompson<\/a> will say it best in his remarkable novel “Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas<\/a>”: “There is nothing in the world more helpless and irresponsible and depraved than a man in the depths of an ether binge.” <\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
