Don\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t they know that all trains are capable of is buckling and derailment? Wait til they read the papers on March 4 to see what happened to Helen Gil. How many budding Cherryl Brookses will leap from this bridge?
<\/p>\n

We here at 1947project know only danger and distress whilst bringing you danger and distress, so I took it upon myself to risk riding the Griffith Park \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cChoo\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d-\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cchoo\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d and after making certain we weren\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t carrying chlorine gas (though I wasn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t sure some of those kids didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t have Sarin on them), I boarded, uncertain that there was to be no repeat of Nowy Dwor, 1949; there was not. I was still nervous, though. That whole Auschwitz thing has given me an aversion to mass transit.<\/p>\n

Here we are in the Floyd & Sam\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s tunnel. Thinking Salerno, 1944.
<\/p>\n

And crossing the bridge:

I thought of the train that plunged off just such a bridge into the Baghmati River, killing 500. The driver had braked to avoid hitting a cow. So here I was. Praying our driver wasn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t Hindu. (Actually, he and I stood around after and shot the bull about the B-24s that took off from Atwater, and the nearby Rancho stables, and his hopping on the Glendale Red Car to go see movies downtown as a child\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dyou\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2d go to Broadway, I asked, heck no, he replied, you go to Main<\/span> Street, and see movies at places where you sat on old crates.) <\/p>\n

So that\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s the tale of the Griffith Park Train. One last note. I don\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t know why, but rolling past these things made me think of some lonely children\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s cemetery.
