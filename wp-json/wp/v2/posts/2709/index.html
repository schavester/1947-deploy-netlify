June 18, 1947
El Monte<\/a><\/p>\n

After ten years, Lester Jean Burnett is tired of the lies. In 1937, aged 18, he acted as a beard for his father Lester Senior, then 36, and Angelina Pizzuto, then 17, whose parents objected to their February-July romance.<\/p>\n

Angelina and Lester Junior were wed, but it was Lester Senior who set up house with the girl. Angelina\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s children Lester Bryan, 8, and Rose, 6, are supported by and informally acknowledged as the children of the older man. However, on their birth certificates, Lester Junior is named father.<\/p>\n

In December 1942, all parties convened in Reno, where Angelina and Lester Junior divorced; Angelina and Lester Senior were married the same day.<\/p>\n

Lester Junior entered Superior Court today seeking an official ruling that the two children are not his.<\/p>\n

Lester Senior, a refrigeration engineer, resides at 2135 Iola Ave., El Monte, with Angelina and the kids.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
