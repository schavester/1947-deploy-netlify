October 4, 1947
Long Beach<\/p>\n

James Harry Hoxworth, 40, has a pretty good idea who’s messing with his wife Violet–family friend Harold E. Ward, also 40. Why, they’re out in a car together right now!<\/p>\n

James and his shotgun waited at the Hoxworth home at 1143-B Junipero Ave<\/a>., and when Harold and Violet pulled up, James jammed the barrel through the car window and blew a fatal hole in Harold’s chest. Then he yanked Violet out the passenger door and beat her soundly.<\/p>\n

When police arrived, they found a note inside the home, scrawled in crayon, in which James accused Violet of infidelity.<\/p>\n
