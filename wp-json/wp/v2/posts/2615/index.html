August 24, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

On Saturday night, officers B.J. Daily and R. W. Douglas broke up a fight between a couple who were brawling in the middle of Santa Monica Blvd. at Purdue Ave.<\/a> The man had a gash between his eyes, and both seemed drunk, so while they seemed a little more refined than most of the lushes, down they went to Lincoln Heights Jail with the rest.<\/a>
Turns out \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201crefined\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d is the right word\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u009dthe gent was John D. Spreckels III, 36, debt-ridden heir to the sugar fortune, the lady his 37-year-old wife. Lou Dell Spreckels denied that she was drunk, explaining that they were fighting because she had refused to give John money to go gambling, and so he had struck her. \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cWhat are you going to do when someone starts hitting you? So we had a fight.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Makes sense to us!<\/p>\n

Both gave their address as a motel at 2500 Santa Monica Blvd. They were each released on a $20 bond and ordered to appear in court at 2pm today to answer the public intoxication charge.<\/p>\n
