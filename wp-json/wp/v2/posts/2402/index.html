We direct our readers to information<\/a> on an event hosted by the LA Press Club in Hollywood on Thursday, a reading and reception for Donald H. Wolfe’s new book The Black Dahlia Files: The Mob, the Mogul, and Murder that Transfixed Los Angeles<\/a> (Regen Books). Entrance, parking and drinks are free.<\/p>\n

The 1947project bloggers have not yet read Mr. Wolfe’s book, but our Larry Harnisch has thoroughly examined the D.A.’s files that form the heart of Wolfe’s thesis. He is concerned that there are numerous inconsistancies between the files he read and Wolfe’s claims (see below). Still, it sounds like an entertaining night out in Miss Short’s old stomping grounds, and we invite any reader who attends to post back in the comments about their experience.<\/p>\n

Larry says: Having been through the D.A.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s files on the Dahlia case (I inventoried and indexed every scrap of paper) I can say definitively that Bugsy Siegel, Norman Chandler, Jack Dragna and Brenda Allen are never mentioned. <\/span> <\/p>\n

In fact, the D.A.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s files say 1) Elizabeth Short was not a prostitute and 2) she wasn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t in Los Angeles in 1944 or 1945. According to the D.A.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s files and everything else I have ever found, she arrived in late July or early August 1946. <\/p>\n

I chatted with Wolfe while he was going through the files at the D.A.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s office so I know he had the opportunity to find those documents. He apparently just ignored them. <\/p>\n

The D.A.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s files, incidentally, aren\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t some neat archive. They\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2re papers that were saved when the D.A. was cleaning house and were just shoveled into boxes. Material from different cases is mixed up, including unlabeled photographs from other murders that got put into the Dahlia material\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u0153at least one turns up incorrectly identified in a certain \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201ctrue\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d crime book. <\/p>\n

Siegel\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s voluminous FBI files are online at the bureau\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s FOIA site. They are heavily censored but show he was under virtually constant surveillance from at least the middle of 1946. Agents saw him (and Virginia Hill) move out of the Chateau Marmont and into the house on Linden on Jan. 14, 1947, and files say he and Hill left for Palm Springs the next day. I refer specifically to FBI document 62-81518-406 and surrounding material. <\/p>\n

I just got the book and haven\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t read it yet as I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2m getting ready for the Crime Bus tour this weekend, but anything that relies on \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cSevered,\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d which is 25% mistakes and 50% fiction, cannot be trusted. I\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ll put on my waders and slog through this opus when I get a chance. <\/p>\n
