–no. I stand by my estimation of 19 December (uh, and of 21 December) wherein I take nursing to task. At the risk of conjuring the ire of the American Nursing Association, I put it to y’all, that Lucille \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cRegistered Nurse\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d Bianchi\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s auto was somehow bewitched by said\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s nursing powers. That which can be equated with nursing invariably invokes madness, mayhem and death. Prove me wrong, ANA? Didn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t think so.<\/p>\n

Bianchi\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s auto. An inanimate object animates itself<\/span>. That\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s hard to do. Under the direction of a nurse, however\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u00a6<\/p>\n

Yeah yeah, leave nurses alone. Nurses see more death than doctors. Hell, nurses see more death than do most Army sergeants. The only gals who see more death than nurses are lady morticians.<\/p>\n

(Those of you who, like this humble writer, only date nurses and morticians know that one cannot, under any circumstances, put both in the same room at the same time. Neither oil and water, nor fire and ice, between the two, it\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s fire and another kind<\/span> of fire\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u00a6 but on the Crazy Scale, nurses outstrip morticians 11-5.
