\nFeb. 4, 2007
\nSouth Pasadena <\/p>\n

The Times publishes three architectural drawings of \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cartistic bungalows\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d prepared by the firm of Wilson and Barnes. One is being built by W.E. Fox on Columbia near Sunset Boulevard, the second by Dr. T.H. Lowers on Main Street in Alhambra and the third by A.J. Padau on Marengo in South Pasadena \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cnear the Monrovia car line.\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d <\/p>\n

<\/o:p><\/p>\n

The Times says of Padau\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s home: \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c5\u201cThis, perhaps, is the best located of the three houses, as from its windows can be seen the entire panorama of mountain and valley to the north and east. It is strictly modern in its design. A feature of the exterior is the broad span from corner to corner of the porch, affording an unobstructed view from the large living room in the front of the house. There are five rooms in the little structure. The cost was $2,500 ($51,308.93 USD 2005).\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00c2\u009d\n<\/p>\n
