November 12, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Mrs. R.J. Odman was sleeping peacefully in her bed at 825 N. Wilcox Ave.<\/a> when Harry Lavine, 41-year-old guest of her upstairs neighbor, actor Matty Fain<\/a>, shot himself just below the heart. The bullet exited Lavine’s back, came through floor and ceiling, and passed through Mrs. Odman’s splayed hair before stopping.<\/p>\n

She was startled but unharmed; Lavine is in serious condition in the Prison Ward of General Hospital. Not for the threat to Mrs. Odman, though: Lavine, also an actor, was out on $5000 bail on a narcotics charge, but had failed to appear in federal court on Monday. Prior to shooting himself, he left a note absolving Margie Martini, 28, who was arrested with him on the drug charge. Miss Martini awaits her preliminary hearing in the County Jail.<\/p>\n

Dead Reckoning<\/a>, with Humphrey Bogart and Lizbeth Scott (Fain, uncredited, as “Ed”)
