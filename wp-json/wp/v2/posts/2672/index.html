July 13, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

When Mrs. Dorothea Lee, 35, left husband Horace last May 9, she might have thought she was rid of him. But Horace wasn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t finished with Dorothea. Last night, there were noises in the backyard of 559 W. 90th Street<\/a>, disturbing the dog. Dorothea\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s parents were visiting from Portland, so her father, George C. Brooks, went out to see what it was. He was promptly felled by three shots to the head. Dorothea and her mother saw Horace standing over him and ran to a neighbor\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s to call the police. Then they went back to the house (ah, such innocent times). There on the living room floor with a bullet in the head, the estranged Horace. Brooks died on the scene, but his son-in-law lingers. They\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2ve moved him to the prison ward at General Hospital for now, and if he lives will do what they can to kill him.
