July 7, 1947
Los Angeles<\/p>\n

Who will ever understand the mind of a woman?<\/p>\n

Nellie Robison was chased screaming from her home at 129 West Century Blvd.<\/a> last night by husband Frank, 48-year-old welder. She was smart to run: Frank was firing .22-caliber bullets. Nellie found safety at a neighbor’s house, and cops finally rousted her old man after firing tear gas canisters (and gassing themselves on the upwind). Once Frank was cuffed, Nellie ran out and embraced him, calling him “Honey Boy,” and urging him to go along with the officers. Frank and his red-eyed captors adjourned to Georgia Street Receiving Hospital for eyewashes, and Frank was later booked at 77th Street Station on suspicion of assault with a deadly weapon.<\/p>\n

We’ll assume his sweet Nellie will be waiting when, and if, he gets out.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
