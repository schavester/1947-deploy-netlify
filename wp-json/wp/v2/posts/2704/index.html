June 21, 1947
Lincoln Heights<\/p>\n

Mrs. Ruth Twist wasn\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2t planning on a wild animal encounter when she entered the alley behind her home at 2219 Johnson Street, facing 2226 Griffin Street.<\/a> But there she discovered an 1800 pound steer, anxious, exhausted and far from its unhappy recent home in a packing house near 700 N. Alameda.<\/p>\n

That\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s where the animal was first spotted, skidding on the pavement. Police tracked the beast from Alameda and Main, but lost it crossing Griffin<\/a>, where it knocked Officer Herbert Hansford down.<\/p>\n

Then the animal entered the alley, there pinning Mrs. Twist against a fence. The brave lady forced the steer to the alley\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s mouth, where officers commenced firing, felling the beast and sending Officer L.P. Walters to Georgia Street Receiving Hospital with a ricocheted bullet to the arm. Mrs. Ruth Twist refused medical attention. And Chief Horrall had steak for dinner.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
