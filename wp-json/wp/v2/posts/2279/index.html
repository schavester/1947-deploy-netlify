\nMarch 3, 1927
\nLos Angeles\n<\/p>\n

\n"Who can take a sunrise,
\nSprinkle it with dew,
\nCover it in chocolate and a miracle or two?
\nThe candyman, the candyman can,
\nThe candyman can ’cause he mixes it with love and makes the world taste good."\n<\/p>\n

\nWhen local school children crave a hand full of gum drops and a pint of whiskey, where can they go? Rumor has it that if they visit Frank Belioi\u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u201e\u00a2s candy store at 5973 South Broadway<\/a>, they may leave with a few new cavities and a major buzz.\n<\/p>\n

\nSgt. Childers was in charge of the squad that raided the local sweets shop, and revealed in court that although the police department had reports that Frank was selling liquor to minors, they had failed to produce evidence against him.\n<\/p>\n

\nFrank was lucky \u00c3\u00a2\u00e2\u201a\u00ac\u00e2\u20ac\u0153 the only thing the cops managed to bust him for was the one and one-half gallons of whiskey on the premises. He said he kept it on hand for party guests.\n<\/p>\n

\nFrank pleaded guilty to the possession charge, and Judge Ambrose fined the candy man $300 [$3,639.31 current USD].\n<\/p>\n
