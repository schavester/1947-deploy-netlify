May 1, 1927
Los Angeles <\/p>\n

In the Times<\/em> today, a round-up of street crime incidents calculated to terrorize city residents, or at least discourage freelance musicians, good Samaritans and lingering outside a lady’s home in an open car–sheesh, buddy, get a room. <\/p>\n

Clarinetist Antonio Cili thought he was being hired to play a gig when three gentlemen picked him up at Sixth and Broadway, drove<\/a> to Fourth and Pecan, tossed him from the car, beat him silly and stole his instrument and $20.<\/p>\n

Jennie Emerson of 2611 Vallejo Street was nearly run down in the street while crossing at Daly and Manitou<\/a> in Lincoln Heights, and while recovering her wits confronted by the armed driver and his pal, who threatened to kill her before stealing her purse.<\/p>\n

A bandit robbed J. Maganuma of $40 cash and a serving of chop suey at his restaurant at 4911 South Broadway<\/a>. It was not reported if Mr. Maganuma spat in the food, but we certainly hope so.<\/p>\n

A. Eisner was carjacked at First and New Hampshire, forced to drive<\/a> to Sixth and Lucas and relieved of his $100 stick pin, $40 watch and $8 cash. Maybe it’s Eisner’s home address of 5579 Santa Monica Boulevard<\/a> or the fancy stick pin that gives this brief tale the whiff of rough trade, or possibly we just have dirty minds.<\/p>\n

Joseph Michael, while strolling by a doorway near First and Main <\/a>was lassoed by a couple of rope-wielding miscreants who strangled Michael into unconsciousness and stole $35, this just two blocks from Central Police HQ. <\/p>\n

Kindly Arthur Roper was driving along (now defunct) California Street near Figueroa when he spied a fashionably garbed young lady in apparent distress in the middle of the road. He stopped to lend aid and her friend hopped onto Roper’s running board with a revolver, which was clapped to Roper’s chest while the gal riffled his pockets of $53 cash. <\/p>\n

And then there was Jacob L. Johannes of 228 South Rodeo Drive, who was sitting in a car with Miss Marie Boucher outside her home at 5806 Carlton Way<\/a> when a fiend with a revolver relieved the lady of a $1000 fur coat, $75 watch and $50 bar pin. Johannes lost $6 cash. Buddy, you can’t afford<\/em> a room… or Miss Boucher.  <\/p>\n

Now be careful out there! <\/p>\n
